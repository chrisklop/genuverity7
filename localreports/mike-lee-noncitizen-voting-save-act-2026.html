TL;DR — VERDICT: FALSE

Sen. Mike Lee's "hundreds of thousands" of noncitizen voters is a number he invented on live television.

When asked directly on Fox News how many noncitizens voted illegally in 2020 and 2024, Lee admitted: "I don't know those numbers." He then improvised a speculative ceiling of "at least tens of thousands, probably hundreds of thousands" with no data source. Utah's exhaustive, months-long audit of 2,069,640 voter registrations found exactly one noncitizen — who had never voted. The Heritage Foundation's own fraud database documents 99 total confirmed noncitizen voting cases in 44 years across all 50 states. Lee's minimum estimate (200,000) exceeds that lifetime total by a factor of more than 2,000. The federal SAVE verification tool he cites as evidence has a documented false-positive rate exceeding 50% in Missouri's Boone County.

Executive Summary

Sen. Mike Lee (R-UT) has made "hundreds of thousands of noncitizen voters" the statistical centerpiece of his SAVE America Act — a bill requiring documentary proof of citizenship for all federal voter registrations. When pressed for evidence during a Fox News interview on March 23, 2026, Lee conceded he did not actually know the numbers, then fabricated a ceiling estimate of "hundreds of thousands" while citing a federal matching tool — the DHS SAVE (Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements) program — that has been independently documented to wrongly flag naturalized U.S. citizens as noncitizens at alarming rates. [1]

The SAVE program's own track record reveals a Missouri county where more than half of all flagged voters were confirmed U.S. citizens, and a Texas review where at least 14 percent of flagged voters were citizens. The 10,000 cases the program identified from 49.5 million records checked — a rate of 0.02 percent — are almost certainly an overcount, not an undercount, of actual noncitizens. [2] [3]

The most rigorous test of Lee's hypothesis comes from his own state. Utah's Republican Lt. Governor Deidre Henderson oversaw an exhaustive, months-long review of all 2,069,640 active voter registrations. The result: exactly one apparent noncitizen on the rolls, who had never voted and was promptly removed. Henderson herself called the federal SAVE database "notoriously inaccurate." The Heritage Foundation's own Election Fraud Database contains only 99 total confirmed cases of noncitizen voting since 1982. Lee's "hundreds of thousands" claim exceeds the Heritage Foundation's documented lifetime total by a factor of more than 1,000. [4] [5] [6]

After weeks of Senate floor debate, Senate Majority Leader John Thune pivoted the chamber away from the SAVE America Act on April 22, 2026, citing more urgent deadline-driven legislation. A Kennedy amendment to attach the bill to a reconciliation package was defeated 48–50, with four Republicans — Murkowski, McConnell, Collins, and Tillis — joining all Democrats in opposition. The bill stalled, unable to reach either the 60-vote filibuster threshold or the 51-vote reconciliation threshold. Lee vowed to revive it. The legislative record is unambiguous: the SAVE Act was built on a manufactured statistical threat. [7] [8]

Section 1: The Claim — What Mike Lee Said

The "hundreds of thousands" figure did not emerge from any study, audit, or federal investigation. It was improvised by Lee during a Fox News "Sunday Futures" interview with Maria Bartiromo on March 23, 2026. When Bartiromo asked how many people voted illegally in the 2020 and 2024 elections, Lee explicitly acknowledged ignorance first: "I don't know those numbers." He then pivoted to registration rather than actual voting, and offered a speculative ceiling: "What I do believe is that there are at least tens of thousands probably hundreds of thousands by the time all the research is done of people who have registered to vote in elections in one state or another." [9]

This is not a data-driven estimate. It is a guess manufactured on live television, then promoted as factual justification for a sweeping federal mandate. Bartiromo — who, according to Media Matters for America, spread noncitizen voting conspiracy theories more than 20 times during 2024 alone — did not challenge the claim or ask for its source. The interview went viral and was cited in White House briefings and Senate floor speeches as evidence of the need for the SAVE Act. [9]

Lee repeated the claim on the Senate floor during weeks of debate in March and April 2026, characterizing noncitizen voting as diluting "one of our most sacred, fundamental rights." The Gateway Pundit, Pravda USA, and KUTV (Salt Lake City) all reported Lee's estimate without qualification. The White House's dedicated "SAVE America" webpage framed the SAVE Act as an emergency response to a verified threat. At no point did any of these outlets or the White House cite a peer-reviewed study, government audit, or named statistical source for the "hundreds of thousands" figure.

The claim exploited several structural vulnerabilities: with an estimated 30 million noncitizens in the U.S., the idea that some might accidentally or intentionally register feels intuitively plausible without context. Proving the negative — that hundreds of thousands of noncitizens are NOT registered — requires laborious state-by-state audits that take months. The claim takes seconds to make. And Harvard CAPS/Harris polling found 91 percent of Republican base voters support the SAVE Act, making the underlying claim politically cost-free to propagate even in the absence of evidence.

Section 2: The Evidence — What State Investigations Actually Found

Every systematic, government-conducted or peer-reviewed investigation of noncitizen voting in the modern era has produced findings that are inconsistent with Lee's claim by orders of magnitude. The most thorough comes from Lee's own state. Utah Lt. Gov. Deidre Henderson — a Republican — oversaw an exhaustive, months-long review of all 2,069,640 active voter registrations, cross-referencing driver's license records, federal immigration databases, and manual verification. The result: exactly one apparent noncitizen on the rolls — who had never voted and was promptly removed. Hundreds of other flagged records reflected missing information or data errors, not illegal registrations. Henderson's conclusion: "The bottom line is, there is not a widespread problem." [4] [5]

The Heritage Foundation's Election Fraud Database — the most comprehensive conservative repository of alleged election fraud, maintained by an organization with every institutional incentive to document as many cases as possible — contains only 99 total confirmed cases of noncitizen voting since 1982. That is 44 years, all 50 states, an average of 2.3 cases per year. Lee's conservative minimum of 200,000 exceeds that lifetime total by a factor of more than 2,000, with zero empirical basis provided. [6]

Other recent state investigations reinforce the picture:

  • Michigan 2024 audit: 15 apparent noncitizen ballots out of 5.7 million cast (0.00028 percent). Michigan Secretary Benson: "This tiny fraction doesn't justify laws blocking tens of thousands." [15]
  • Iowa 2024 audit: 35 confirmed noncitizens who voted out of 1.6 million ballots — an initial claim of 2,176 potential noncitizens shrank to 277 confirmed, then to 35 after full investigation. The initial number was off by a factor of 62. [12]
  • Georgia 2024 audit: 9 noncitizens found to have voted out of 8.2 million registered voters. [15]
  • Oregon: 38 noncitizens voted out of 2.3 million ballots. [15]
  • Wyoming: 13 voters flagged — all 13 proved legally eligible to vote. [15]
  • Texas: 33 potential noncitizen voters out of 11.3 million 2024 ballots; no charges filed as of late July 2025. [15]
  • Brennan Center survey of 2016 election officials: 30 suspected noncitizen incidents out of 23.5 million ballots cast (0.0001 percent). [13]
  • New York Times survey after 2016: 2 possible instances of noncitizen voting among 137.7 million voters nationwide. [13]

The Center for Election Innovation & Research identified a consistent investigative pattern: "Initial claims of large numbers shrink dramatically after proper investigation. When officials conduct thorough reviews, flagged records are revised downward by 80–90 percent." [12] USCIS data shows that only 0.04 percent of voter verification cases are returned as confirmed noncitizens — meaning in the 49.5 million records Lee cited, the most generous USCIS-consistent projection would yield roughly 19,800 potential noncitizens among registered voters — not 200,000 minimum, not "hundreds of thousands," and still not verified fraud.

Section 3: The Broken Tool — SAVE System's False Positive Crisis

Lee and the White House have repeatedly cited the DHS SAVE program's findings — approximately 10,000 potential noncitizen matches from 49.5 million voter registrations — as corroboration for the "hundreds of thousands" claim. This inverts the evidence. The 10,000 figure is a raw computer match, not a verified count of noncitizens. SAVE works by cross-referencing voter rolls against Social Security Administration data and USCIS immigration records. The fatal flaw: SSA citizenship data is frequently outdated or incomplete for people born outside the U.S. who received Social Security numbers before naturalizing — meaning tens of millions of naturalized Americans can be flagged as noncitizens before the system updates. USCIS acknowledged this directly: "SAVE doesn't have access to all potential data that could show whether someone is a citizen." [3] [21]

The documented error rates in pilot states are staggering:

  • Missouri's Boone County: More than half of 74 flagged voters were confirmed U.S. citizens. Boone County Clerk Brianna Lennon refused to act on the data: "This is not ready for prime time. And I'm not going to risk the security and the constitutional rights of my voters for bad data." One of the flagged voters' registration forms bore initials from a staff member who had helped him register at his naturalization ceremony — meaning SAVE flagged him precisely because the citizenship update had not yet propagated through the database. [2]
  • Missouri statewide: An initial list of 1,200+ flagged voters in seven populous counties was the basis for state officials directing county administrators to render those voters "temporarily ineligible" — before the data was shown to be wrong. [2]
  • Denton County, Texas: 14 percent of flagged voters (12 of 84) proved to be citizens. [2]
  • Travis County, Texas: 11 of 97 flagged voters were confirmed citizens (11 percent); an additional 25 percent "had already provided proof of citizenship when registering to vote" — meaning SAVE failed to integrate documentation those voters had submitted to the same government. Travis County voter registrar Celia Israel: "It has proven to be inaccurate. Why would I rely on it?" [2]
  • Charlotte County, Florida: Of 15 voters flagged from 176,000 records, 3 were mistakenly added and 2 others had already documented their naturalized citizenship — a confirmed false-positive rate of at least 33 percent. [21]
  • Texas — Potter County: 9 flagged, 3 confirmed citizens (33 percent). [2]
  • DHS correction record: Federal officials had to correct information provided to at least five states after SAVE misidentified citizens as noncitizens. [3]

USCIS official David Jennings, defending the tool's rapid deployment, stated: "We tested it and deployed it to our users in two weeks." The speed of that deployment is precisely the problem. Campaign Legal Center's Danielle Lang warned: "This data will be reliably stale and will target naturalized citizens for undue suspicion." As of March 2026, over 70 voting agencies across at least 24 states are using SAVE for voter eligibility — up from a handful of pilot states in late 2025 — meaning the documented false-positive rates are now affecting voter roll decisions in states representing tens of millions of registrations. [21] [1]

Key Technical Flaw

The core problem is a 40-year gap in SSA citizenship records. People who received Social Security numbers before naturalizing may lack any citizenship flag in the SSA data that SAVE queries — even though they are fully confirmed U.S. citizens. The system's architecture guarantees it will disproportionately flag naturalized Americans, who are concentrated in immigrant-heavy urban and suburban communities. [21]

Section 4: What the SAVE Act Actually Does

H.R. 22 — the SAVE America Act — passed the House on February 11, 2026, and was sent to the Senate for consideration. Its core provisions, drawn directly from the bill text, are far more sweeping than its title suggests: [16]

  • Section 2 — Documentary proof mandate: No state may accept or process a federal voter registration unless the applicant presents documentary proof of U.S. citizenship. Acceptable documents are limited to: a REAL ID-compliant ID indicating citizenship; a valid U.S. passport; a military ID plus service record showing U.S. birthplace; a government-issued photo ID indicating U.S. birthplace; a naturalization or citizenship certificate; or a Consular Report of Birth Abroad.
  • Section 2 — SAVE mandate: States must submit voter rolls to the DHS SAVE database and act on results within 30 days of enactment.
  • Section 3 — Photo ID to vote: Voters must present photo ID to cast ballots in federal elections; mail voters must photocopy ID documents.
  • Section 4 — Private right of action: Any person may sue an election official who registers an applicant who failed to provide documentary proof of citizenship — exposing individual government workers to personal lawsuits by private citizens. Maine Secretary of State Shenna Bellows: election officials must require documentation "under penalty of criminal prosecution." [16] [18]
  • Section 5 — Federal data sharing: Any federal agency must provide citizenship-relevant information to state election officials within 24 hours of request.
  • Re-registration scope: The requirement applies whenever a voter updates their registration — after moving, changing name, or switching party — meaning millions of already-registered Americans would need to re-comply.

The National Association of Counties estimated the legislation would impose approximately $510 million in additional costs per election cycle — representing 11.3 times the current federal HAVA funding of $45 million for FY 2026 — without providing any dedicated federal funding. Between 770,000 and 1.2 million poll workers and election administrators would require training, demanding 2.5 million to 5 million additional training hours in the first implementation cycle alone. NACo identified seven major implementation obstacles: unfunded mandates, federalism conflicts, insufficient timelines, citizenship verification errors with REAL ID, criminal penalties discouraging election workers, training demands, and risks to eligible voter access. [17]

The documented precedent is alarming. Kansas implemented a similar proof-of-citizenship requirement for voter registration and it blocked approximately 31,000 eligible citizens — roughly 12 percent of all applicants — from registering, while finding only 39 noncitizen registrations over 20 years. The ratio: approximately 795 eligible citizens disenfranchised for every one noncitizen registration prevented. The Bipartisan Policy Center confirmed: "USCIS verification data shows just 0.04% of voter verification cases are returned as noncitizens." [10]

Eight Secretaries of State explicitly called the bill unnecessary, harmful, or suppressive. California Secretary of State Shirley Weber: "The SAVE Act would disenfranchise millions of eligible voters, including married women, veterans, low-income Americans, people of color, and young voters." Minnesota Secretary of State Steve Simon: "The SAVE Act would be a disaster for American elections, and could shut out tens of millions of eligible American voters (these are U.S. citizens) who won't be able to comply." Contra Costa County Clerk-Recorder Kristin Connelly, summing up the consensus: "The Safeguard American Voter Eligibility Act claims to solve a problem that doesn't exist." [18]

Section 5: Evidence Deep-Dive — SAVE Act Senate Failure & Political Context

The SAVE Act has now failed to advance twice in the Senate. The first attempt relied on breaking the filibuster — with Senate Republicans holding 53 seats, the 60-vote threshold required 7 Democratic crossovers, and documented Democratic support remained at zero. The second and most realistic attempt involved attaching the SAVE Act to a filibuster-proof reconciliation bill. Sen. John Kennedy (R-LA) sponsored an amendment to attach the SAVE America Act to a vote-a-rama session. The amendment was defeated 48–50 on approximately April 23, 2026, with four Republicans joining all Democrats in voting no: [20] [22]

  • Lisa Murkowski (R-AK)
  • Mitch McConnell (R-KY)
  • Susan Collins (R-ME)
  • Thom Tillis (R-NC)

Sen. Thom Tillis had earlier told reporters the bill had a "0% [chance] of succeeding" via filibuster change. Senate Majority Leader Thune pivoted the chamber away from the SAVE Act on April 22, 2026, citing more urgent deadline-driven legislation, saying he would "see about getting back on it" only after more pressing legislation was resolved. Lee threatened to "nuke" the filibuster entirely. Trump separately called for "terminating" the filibuster and vowed not to sign other legislation until the SAVE Act passed — a threat he later retreated from. [7]

The partisan gap is not simply Democrat vs. Republican; it reflects a Republican establishment that views the SAVE Act as an electoral liability in competitive states. The four Republican defectors represent precisely the kinds of states where blocking voter registration carries real political risk. NBC News documented that "some Republicans warn Trump's SAVE America Act is doomed to fail" — a concern rooted not in principle but in competitive vulnerability. [22]

Civil rights and voting rights organizations declared a legislative victory. The Campaign Legal Center, SPLC, and NAACP LDF all noted this was the second time the SAVE Act had failed to advance. SPLC senior policy advisor Laura Williamson: "We are glad to see the SAVE Act stall in the Senate once again." Sen. Alex Padilla (D-CA) told Politico Democrats "won the battle" but Republicans were "not giving up on the war." [8]

Section 6: Contemporary Context — Electoral Strategy & Current Status

The noncitizen voting narrative has been explicitly framed by its primary sponsors as an electoral strategy, not merely a security response. Trump stated in a February 19, 2026 speech in Rome, Georgia: "We'll never lose a race. For 50 years, we won't lose a race" if the SAVE Act passes. He separately told House Republicans the bill would "guarantee the midterms." Senate Majority Leader Thune, navigating between Trump's demands and legislative reality, told NBC: "I understand the president's got a passion to see this issue addressed, as we all do... I can guarantee the debate. I can guarantee a vote. I just can't guarantee an outcome." [20] [22]

LSE political scientist Caitlin Lewis analyzed the explicit electoral framing: Trump's rhetoric "operates from a sense of electoral insecurity, which in turn generates outrage that is then used to justify stricter voting measures." The Heritage Foundation documented only 23 instances of noncitizen voting between 2003 and 2022 — less than two per year in an era of over 130 million annual voters. The explicit electoral framing confirms the claim is not evidence-driven but strategically constructed to reshape the electorate ahead of 2026 midterms. [23]

As of May 1, 2026, the SAVE America Act has no viable path to passage in the current Senate configuration. Lee is not facing reelection in 2026; his current term runs through January 3, 2029, giving him unusual freedom to pursue the SAVE Act as a long-term ideological project. A February 2026 Deseret News/Hinckley Institute poll found 47 percent of Utah voters approve of Lee's Senate performance vs. 37 percent who disapprove — a narrower margin than his standing among Republicans specifically (61 percent approve). Lee has positioned himself as one of Trump's top congressional allies, making the SAVE Act campaign central to his national profile-building. [7]

State-level parallel tracks have continued. Republican governors in Florida, Mississippi, South Dakota, and Utah have signed state-level versions of the SAVE Act into law. Florida's version triggered immediate lawsuits after Gov. DeSantis signed it in early April 2026. Meanwhile, LSE's Lewis documented that the legislation would disproportionately suppress voting among groups already facing barriers: approximately 21.3 million Americans lack ready access to citizenship documents (9 percent of the voting-age population); this rises to 24 percent of Americans under 30 and approximately 50 percent of Black Americans in that age group. Among Americans with household income below $50,000, only 1 in 5 hold a valid passport. An estimated 69 million women cannot present a birth certificate matching their current legal name due to name changes at marriage. [23] [8]

Washington Secretary of State Steve Hobbs — a Republican-appointed official — attended the State of the Union specifically to sound the alarm, stating: "The SAVE America Act doesn't save anything" and calling it "voter disenfranchisement masquerading as election security" that "would make voting harder for eligible citizens while failing to address the real risks election officials face." [19]

Section 9: Conclusion

The "hundreds of thousands of noncitizen voters" claim is, in Lee's own words, something he "believe[s]" — not something he knows, not something any audit has found, not something any peer-reviewed study supports. The Heritage Foundation, working to document as much election fraud as possible, found 99 confirmed noncitizen voting cases in 44 years. Lee's minimum estimate of 200,000 exceeds that by a factor of 2,020 with zero empirical foundation.

The SAVE tool Lee relies on as corroboration has been shown to wrongly flag U.S. citizens at rates ranging from 11 percent to more than 50 percent in the locations that bothered to independently verify the data. The election officials who know the tool best — from Missouri to Texas to Florida to Utah — have called it "not ready for prime time," "inaccurate," and "notoriously inaccurate." The federal agency that built it acknowledged it "doesn't have access to all potential data that could show whether someone is a citizen." [2] [3] [4] [21]

The SAVE Act is not a proportionate response to a documented threat. It is a legislative architecture built around a fabricated statistic, relying on a broken verification tool, that by the Kansas precedent would disenfranchise approximately 795 eligible citizens for every noncitizen registration it prevents — at a cost of $510 million per election cycle in unfunded mandates. Trump's own framing confirms the intent: this is about "guaranteeing the midterms," not protecting election integrity. [10] [17] [20]

The verdict is unambiguous: FALSE. The claim is demonstrably unsupported by evidence and is contradicted by every systematic state investigation conducted in the modern era, including one conducted in Lee's own state, overseen by a Republican Lt. Governor, using precisely the federal tools Lee claims support his estimate. The "hundreds of thousands" figure is not a data point. It is a political instrument, manufactured on live television and deployed to justify a bill whose documented purpose — in the words of its principal sponsor — is to reshape the electorate, not to protect it.
