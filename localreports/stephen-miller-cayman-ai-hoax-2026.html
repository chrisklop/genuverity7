Executive Summary

In late March 2026, a viral claim swept Facebook, Threads, and TikTok alleging that Republican U.S. Rep. Thomas Massie of Kentucky had publicly exposed senior White House adviser Stephen Miller for secretly funneling $890 million into offshore Cayman Islands accounts through a shell company with no employees and no physical office. The claim cited a YouTube video purportedly showing Massie confronting Miller in a congressional hearing, with Miller allegedly “refusing to answer for 101 seconds.” Snopes investigated and rated it False, tracing it to a March 22, 2026, AI-generated YouTube video uploaded by a channel that specializes in fabricating “inspiring or shocking stories about public figures.” [1][2]

Forensic examination revealed multiple telltale markers of AI synthesis: voiceover audio was inconsistent with on-screen closed captions; unnatural vocal glitches occurred throughout; the footage was stitched from an unrelated September 2025 congressional hearing and a March 5, 2026, Miller White House briefing; and YouTube itself applied an “altered or synthetic content” label. Critically, the video’s script falsely identified Massie as a House Oversight Committee member — a committee he is definitively not on in the 119th Congress (2025–2026), where his assignments are the Judiciary Committee and the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee. [3][4]

Stephen Miller’s actual public financial disclosures, filed with the Office of Government Ethics and published by ProPublica, show a net worth in the range of $1.8 million to $4.8 million, concentrated in domestic equity holdings. No offshore accounts of any kind appear in any disclosed filing. The $890 million figure exists solely in the AI-generated hoax ecosystem. [5][16]

The Viral Claim: "$890 Million, Zero Evidence"

In late March 2026, a video swept across Facebook, Threads, and TikTok carrying a headline that seemed designed to go viral: "'WHO Got Paid?' — Thomas Massie Presses on Bank Records as Stephen Miller Dodges Question for 101 Seconds." The video purported to show Rep. Thomas Massie (R-KY) confronting White House senior adviser Stephen Miller in a congressional hearing, alleging Miller had secretly routed $890 million through a Cayman Islands shell company — a company with no employees and no physical office. [1][2]

The claim spread via comment-section links to ad-monetized AI blog articles, which published written versions of the false narrative to capture search traffic. The propagation pattern — YouTube origin to blog amplification to social sharing loop — proved highly effective at eluding fact-checkers during the critical first 48 hours. [1]

What Miller's Financial Disclosures Actually Show

Stephen Miller's financial disclosures, filed with the Office of Government Ethics and published by ProPublica, tell a very different story. His OGE Form 278e shows a net worth in the range of $1.8 million to $4.8 million — concentrated entirely in domestic equity holdings. His single largest pre-divestiture position was Palantir Technologies stock worth $100,000–$250,000, which he sold on August 14, 2025 to comply with ethics laws. No offshore accounts of any kind appear in any disclosed filing. [5][6][16]

The $890 million figure cited in the hoax is approximately 200 to 500 times Miller's actual estimated net worth. It appears in no government financial record, court document, CIMA database, or credible news report. The figure was fabricated wholesale. [11][17]

Miller's genuine financial controversies are far more modest: POGO documented that his Palantir stock represented a potential conflict of interest given his role overseeing immigration enforcement — the very government work Palantir's data software supports. NOTUS reported Miller received a "golden parachute" of $567,000 from America First Legal in 2024 before rejoining the White House. These are real, documented concerns. They bear no resemblance to the fabricated $890M offshore account narrative. [17][18]
