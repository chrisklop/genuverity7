Executive Summary

In April 2026, a false claim that COVID-19 vaccines killed between 20,000 and 60,000 Germans achieved explosive global reach after Elon Musk amplified it to his 237 million followers on X. The claim originated in testimony given on March 19, 2026, by Dr. Helmut Sterz — a retired veterinarian and former Pfizer Europe toxicologist who left the company in 2007 — before Germany's Bundestag Corona Enquete Commission, where he had been invited to speak by the far-right AfD parliamentary faction. Sterz applied a discredited "underreporting multiplier" of 30 to raw passive surveillance figures from the Paul-Ehrlich-Institut (PEI), inflating 2,133 reported deaths (temporally proximate to vaccination, not confirmed as causally linked) into a projected death toll of approximately 60,000. His methodology, experts have noted, "confuses coincidence with causation, misuses passive surveillance data, and is not supported by the best available scientific evidence." [1] [2]

Far-right Swedish influencer Peter Imanuelsen (PeterSweden) posted Sterz's claims on April 12, 2026, framing them as a bombshell admission from "a Pfizer insider who was former head of toxicology in Europe." Elon Musk reposted the content within minutes, adding a personal anecdote: "my second vaccine shot almost sent me to the hospital. Felt like I was dying." [1] Within 30 hours, Musk's post had accumulated more than 50 million views; by April 16, both posts had collectively reached over 120 million views. [3]

The actual PEI data shows that of 197 million COVID-19 vaccine doses administered in Germany, 2,133 deaths were reported following Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination. Of those, PEI's individual causality assessment confirmed only 28 as having a "possible or probable" causal relationship — a rate of approximately 0.00002% per dose. Pfizer confirmed Sterz had no involvement in COVID-19 vaccine development. Germany's Health Minister Karl Lauterbach rejected Sterz's claims as false during the same parliamentary session. [4] [5] Neither Musk nor Imanuelsen issued corrections.

2. Who Is Dr. Helmut Sterz? The "Pfizer Insider" Who Left in 2007

Imanuelsen's framing of Sterz as "a Pfizer insider" with privileged knowledge of vaccine safety failures is the foundational misrepresentation in this episode. Dr. Helmut Sterz, born in 1946, is a veterinarian by training with a doctorate in virology from Justus Liebig University in Giessen. He worked for pharmaceutical companies including Roche and Pfizer over a 35-year career, heading Pfizer's European toxicology centers in Amboise, France, and Sandwich, UK. [7]

He retired from Pfizer in 2007 — more than 13 years before the COVID-19 pandemic and 14 years before the Pfizer/BioNTech Comirnaty vaccine received conditional EU marketing authorization. Pfizer confirmed unambiguously that Sterz "was not working at Pfizer during the pandemic or during the decade preceding it, and consequently had no involvement in the development of the COVID-19 vaccine." [1] An independent researcher noted online: "The person retired in 2008 and only looked at the published documents (like you and I can do)." [15]

Following his retirement, Sterz authored a book titled Die Impf-Mafia ("The Vaccine Mafia") arguing COVID-19 vaccines were unlawfully approved. [8] He was not called before the Bundestag as an independent expert — he was invited by the AfD, Germany's far-right political party, as part of a deliberate parliamentary strategy to launder fringe claims through the credibility of a formal legislative proceeding. The AfD's Corona Enquete Commission hearings also included international anti-vaccine figures Robert Malone (USA) and Robert Roos (Netherlands). [22] Germany's Health Minister Karl Lauterbach, present at the March 19, 2026 hearing, publicly rejected Sterz's claims during the same session. [6]

3. The Core Distortion: How 28 Deaths Became a "60,000" Claim

Sterz's central death estimate rests on a methodological error that has repeatedly fueled vaccine misinformation across multiple countries and years. Germany's Paul-Ehrlich-Institut operates a passive pharmacovigilance system — a voluntary reporting mechanism that collects adverse events occurring after vaccination. These systems are explicitly designed to detect signals for further investigation, not to establish or quantify causation. [17]

In his testimony, Sterz cited the PEI's figure of approximately 2,133 deaths reported following Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination and applied a "30x underreporting factor" derived from a 2011 Harvard Pilgrim Health Care study (ESP:VAERS) commissioned by the U.S. Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality. [23] This produced his estimate of 20,000–60,000 deaths. The methodology fails on multiple levels:

Wrong system, wrong country: The Harvard Pilgrim study examined U.S. VAERS, not Germany's PEI pharmacovigilance network. Germany operates a more centralized, physician-reporting-oriented system. Reporting rates are not transferable between the two systems. [1]

General events vs. fatal events: The CDC explicitly states that "more serious and unexpected medical events are probably more likely to be reported than minor ones, especially when they occur soon after vaccination." [14] Applying a general underreporting multiplier to fatal events — which are more likely to be reported, not less — is methodologically invalid.

Notoriety bias during COVID rollout: Any death following vaccination during the pandemic received intense public and media attention, making pandemic-era death overreporting more likely than under-reporting. The PEI spokesperson stated directly that applying "a fixed underreporting factor" lacks a valid "data basis." [1]

Temporal correlation treated as causation: All 2,133 temporally proximate deaths were treated as vaccine-caused — a claim the PEI report explicitly rejects. Following individual causality assessment, only 28 of those deaths were classified as having a "possible or probable" causal relationship. [4]

Expert consensus is unambiguous. Dr. Mahmoud Zureik, director of EPI-PHARE and professor at the University of Paris-Saclay, stated the approach "confuses coincidence with causation, misuses passive surveillance data, and is not supported by the best available scientific evidence," calling the factor of 30 "arbitrary." Dr. Jeffrey S. Morris, director of the biostatistics division at the University of Pennsylvania, stated: "It is not valid to presume the 2,000 reported deaths were caused by vaccines, much less to presume that there were 30x this number to arrive at the 60k number the person claims." [1]

7. The Lives-Saved Equation: What the Claim Omitted

The mortality context entirely absent from Sterz's testimony and Musk's amplification of it: COVID-19 vaccines saved vastly more lives than the number even temporarily associated with vaccine-related harm.

A retrospective surveillance study published in The Lancet Respiratory Medicine (2024), analyzing data from 34 WHO European member states from December 2020 to March 2023, estimated that approximately 182,988 deaths were averted in Germany due to COVID-19 vaccines. Germany ranked third in the WHO European Region by absolute number of deaths prevented — behind only the UK and Italy. [24]

Across the WHO European Region, vaccines are estimated to have saved more than 1.4 million lives, reducing COVID-19 mortality by at least 57%. WHO Europe Regional Director Hans Henri P. Kluge stated: "Today, there are more than 1.4 million people in our Region enjoying life with their loved ones because they took the vital decision to be vaccinated." [24]

A September 2025 study published in JAMA Health Forum by researchers at Università Cattolica and Stanford University estimated that COVID-19 vaccines prevented 2.5 million deaths globally between 2020 and 2024, preserving 14.8 million life-years — an efficiency ratio of one death prevented per 5,400 doses administered. [25]

Using PEI's own figure of 74 probable causal deaths across all COVID vaccines and all doses administered in Germany (197 million doses), and the WHO Europe estimate of 182,988 deaths averted: the benefit-to-harm ratio for Germany is approximately 2,473 lives saved for every probable causal death — a ratio of 2,473:1. Sterz's claimed 60,000 deaths, even if credited, would represent roughly one-third of the lives vaccine opponents claim were lost — against 182,988 lives WHO estimates were saved. This context is entirely absent from the viral posts that reached 120 million people.
