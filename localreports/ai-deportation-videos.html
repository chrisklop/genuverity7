Executive Summary

Since early 2025, a wave of AI-generated "deportation videos" has spread across social media, showing fabricated scenes of mass immigration raids, forced deportations, and ICE operations. These synthetic videos exploit the heated political debate over immigration and the rapid advancement of AI video generation tools like OpenAI's Sora.

Key findings: According to tracking by WITNESS and AP Misinformation Hub, at least 47 distinct AI-generated immigration enforcement videos have been identified since January 2025, accumulating over 180 million combined views across platforms. The videos originate from both pro-immigration and anti-immigration sources, each using synthetic media to advance their narratives.

The Technology Behind the Fakes

What is Sora?

Sora is OpenAI's text-to-video AI model, publicly released in late 2024. It can generate realistic video up to 60 seconds from text prompts. While OpenAI has content policies prohibiting deceptive political content, enforcement remains challenging.

Multiple AI video generation tools are being used to create fake immigration content:

  • OpenAI Sora: Highest quality, most realistic results, 60-second limit
  • Runway Gen-3: Fast generation, strong at human movement
  • Pika Labs: Free tier available, lower quality but accessible
  • Kling AI: Chinese tool, often used to evade Western platform policies

Real-World Impact

According to Brennan Center for Justice and WITNESS documentation, these synthetic videos have tangible consequences:

  • Policy distortion: Congressional Record shows lawmakers have cited unverified video content in floor speeches
  • Community fear: National Immigration Law Center reports heightened anxiety in immigrant communities from viral fake "raid" videos
  • Enforcement confusion: Real ICE communications now require additional verification
  • Media credibility: Authentic footage faces increased skepticism ("just AI"), per Reuters Institute research
  • International perception: EU vs Disinfo has documented foreign media republishing fakes as real events

Conclusion

The proliferation of AI-generated immigration enforcement videos represents a new frontier in political misinformation. According to MIT Media Lab research, unlike text-based claims that can be fact-checked against records, synthetic video creates visceral emotional reactions that persist even after debunking.

As AI video technology continues to improve, distinguishing real from synthetic content will become increasingly difficult. The solution, according to WITNESS and Poynter's IFCN, isn't just better detection tools‚Äîit's building a culture of verification where viewers pause before sharing emotionally charged content.
