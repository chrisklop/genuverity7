The viral "secret military orders" narrative is unsubstantiated. However, the public "Mandate for Leadership" explicitly proposes Schedule F reinstatement (stripping protections from 50,000+ federal workers) and expanded use of the Insurrection Act for domestic military deployment.

Executive Summary

In late 2025, social media erupted with claims of a "secret annex" to Project 2025 containing pre-signed military orders for domestic martial law. Our investigation finds zero evidence of such documents. The actual threat is openly documented: Schedule F would reclassify 50,000+ federal employees as "at-will," with veterans comprising 30% of the targeted workforce. The Project also advocates for expanded use of the Insurrection Act for domestic deployment. This report analyzes the viral conflation, the demographic impact of proposed purges, and the documented proposals that pose real constitutional concerns.

1. The "Secret Orders" Conflation

The rumor of "secret military orders" hidden in Project 2025 appears to be a conflation of three distinct entities: (1) The publicly available 900-page "Mandate for Leadership" document [1], (2) Speculative "war game" exercises conducted by unrelated think tanks, and (3) Verified statements from former administration officials regarding the Insurrection Act [6].

While no "secret annex" has been verified by credible sources [7], the public document achieves similar ends: it explicitly advises ensuring "the Department of Defense" aligns strictly with political leadership, removing traditional civil-military firewalls.

2. Schedule F: The Weaponized "Purge"

The most concrete military-adjacent threat in Project 2025 is Schedule F—an executive order originally signed in October 2020 and rescinded in January 2021 [3]. It sought to reclassify career civil servants as "at-will" employees if their roles touched on "policy."

Project 2025 estimates this would apply to up to 50,000 employees [4]. Proponents frame this as "draining the swamp." However, data analysis reveals a critical oversight: the demographic composition of the federal workforce.

CRITICAL CONTEXT

The "Deep State" is largely composed of former military. Veterans receive hiring preference for federal jobs under the Veterans' Preference Act.

3. The Purge Radius: Impact on Veterans

According to the Office of Personnel Management (OPM), approximately 30% of the entire federal workforce are veterans [2]. In agencies like the Department of Defense and Department of Homeland Security, that number rises to nearly 50% [5].

Any mass firing or reclassification of federal employees under Schedule F would statistically result in the largest mass-termination of military veterans in U.S. history. The executive order contains no exemptions for veteran status.

4. The Insurrection Act Loophole

The viral rumors of "martial law" likely stem from Project 2025's robust defense of the Insurrection Act of 1807 [6]. The Project argues that the President has "plenary authority" to deploy active-duty troops domestically to suppress "lawlessness"—a term left intentionally broad.

Unlike the "Secret Orders" theory, this is not hidden. The Mandate argues against the Department of Justice's current interpretation that limits domestic deployment. The proposal suggests moving the Coast Guard under the Department of Defense in times of crisis, or conversely, using DoD assets for domestic border enforcement, blurring Posse Comitatus lines.
