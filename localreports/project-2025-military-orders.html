1. The "Secret Orders" Conflation

In late 2025, a wave of social media posts began claiming that a "secret annex" of Project 2025 had been leaked, containing pre-signed executive orders for military mobilization. Our analysis tracks the origin of these claims to a conflation of three separate entities:

  1. The publicly available "Mandate for Leadership" (900+ pages).
  2. Speculative "War Game" exercises conducted by third-party think tanks (unrelated to Heritage).
  3. Verified statements by former administration officials regarding the Insurrection Act.

While the "secret annex" remains unsubstantiated, the public text contains proposals that achieve similar ends without the need for secrecy. The document explicitly advises the next administration to "ensure that the Department of Defense" aligns strictly with political leadership, removing the traditional firewall between military officers and political appointees.

2. Schedule F: The Weaponized "Purge"

The most concrete military-adjacent threat in Project 2025 is Schedule F. Originally an executive order signed in Oct 2020 (and rescinded in Jan 2021), it sought to reclassify career civil servants as "at-will" employees if their roles touched on "policy."

Project 2025 estimates this would apply to up to 50,000 employees. Proponents call this "draining the swamp." However, data analysis reveals a critical oversight: the demographic composition of the federal workforce.

CRITICAL CONTEXT: The "Deep State" is largely composed of former military. Veterans receive hiring preference for federal jobs.

3. The Purge Radius: Impact on Veterans

According to the Office of Personnel Management (OPM), approximately 30% of the entire federal workforce are veterans. In agencies like the Department of Defense and Department of Homeland Security, that number rises to nearly 50%.

Any mass firing or reclassification of federal employees ("The Purge") would statistically result in the largest mass-termination of military veterans in U.S. history. Schedule F does not include exemptions for veteran status.

4. The Insurrection Act Loophole

The viral rumors of "martial law" likely stem from Project 2025's robust defense of the Insurrection Act of 1807. The Project argues that the President has "plenary authority" to deploy active-duty troops domestically to suppress "lawlessness"—a term left intentionally broad.

Unlike the "Secret Orders" theory, this is not hidden. The Mandate argues against the Department of Justice's current interpretation that limits domestic deployment. The proposal suggests moving the Coast Guard (a domestic law enforcement agency) under the Department of Defense in wartime, or conversely, using DoD assets for domestic border enforcement, blurring the Posse Comitatus lines.
