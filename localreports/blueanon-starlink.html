Executive Summary

Following the 2024 election, a theory emerged on left-leaning social media claiming that Elon Musk's Starlink satellite network could be used to manipulate election results. Posts on X, TikTok, and Reddit alleged that Starlink's global connectivity could be exploited to hack voting machines or alter vote tallies.

The facts: There is no evidence that Starlink has been or could be used to manipulate U.S. elections. U.S. voting machines are certified by the EAC and are not connected to the internet during voting operations. The theory mirrors right-wing election conspiracy theories (like claims about Dominion voting machines) but targets different technology. Both are equally unfounded.

Context: What is "BlueAnon"?

"BlueAnon" is a term coined to describe left-wing conspiracy theories that mirror the structure and logic of right-wing QAnon conspiracies. These include unfounded claims about election fraud (just from a different political perspective), Russia conspiracies beyond what evidence supports, and now tech-based theories targeting figures like Elon Musk.
