Executive Summary

The Viral Claim

"Icy Silence from Climate Doomers as Controversial Study Outlines Possible New Ice Age"

ORIGIN: Breitbart News, Climate Skeptic Media
The Reality

The research referenced is legitimate paleoclimate science studying Milankovitch cycles - orbital patterns that influence Earth's climate over tens of thousands of years. Any potential ice age is 20,000+ years away, while current global warming is happening now and accelerating. Climate skeptic media cherry-picked this study to create confusion and downplay the immediate climate crisis.

The Science Explained

1. What Are Milankovitch Cycles?

Milankovitch cycles are predictable variations in Earth's orbital geometry that affect how much solar radiation reaches our planet. Named after Serbian scientist Milutin Milankovitch, these cycles include three main components:

Eccentricity: The shape of Earth's orbit changes from nearly circular to more elliptical over a 100,000-year cycle. Obliquity: The tilt of Earth's axis varies between 22.1° and 24.5° over a 41,000-year cycle. Precession: Earth's axis wobbles like a spinning top over a 26,000-year cycle.

These cycles have driven the pattern of ice ages and warm periods over the past several million years. Paleoclimate research confirms this relationship through ice core data, sediment records, and orbital calculations.

2. Why Scientists Study Ice Age Cycles

Research into future ice age timing is normal climate science, not a refutation of current global warming. Scientists study these long-term cycles to understand Earth's climate system comprehensively. The IPCC Sixth Assessment Report explicitly states that natural orbital forcing operates on timescales of tens of thousands of years, while human-caused warming is happening over decades.

According to NASA, we are currently in an interglacial period - a warm phase between ice ages. Without human influence, Earth would naturally trend toward another ice age in approximately 20,000 to 50,000 years. However, this has zero bearing on the current warming trend.

How the Study Was Misrepresented

1. Context Stripping

The Breitbart headline and similar climate skeptic coverage engaged in deliberate context stripping. The original research explicitly acknowledges that current anthropogenic warming is unprecedented and operates on a completely different timescale than Milankovitch forcing.

The claim of "icy silence from climate doomers" is particularly misleading. Climate scientists have never disputed ice age cycles - in fact, they discovered them. The IPCC extensively documents natural climate variability, including orbital forcing, volcanic activity, and solar cycles.

2. False Dichotomy

The framing implies that evidence of future ice age cycles contradicts current warming. This is scientifically nonsensical - it's equivalent to saying "because winter will come in six months, summer heat can't be dangerous now."

Critical Misrepresentation

Timescale manipulation: The article conflates a climate process occurring over 20,000+ years with the immediate climate crisis happening over decades. Global temperatures have risen approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times - a rate unprecedented in the paleoclimate record. Any ice age is millennia away and may never occur due to human greenhouse gas emissions altering Earth's energy balance.

3. Data vs. Reality

NOAA climate data (updated December 2025) shows global land and ocean surface temperatures continue to rise at an accelerating rate. The ten warmest years on record have all occurred since 2010. Arctic sea ice extent has declined by approximately 13% per decade since 1979. NASA confirms 2024 was the warmest year in the instrumental record.

These are not predictions or models - they are direct measurements showing the opposite of what an approaching ice age would produce.

Why This Matters

Cherry-picking legitimate climate research to mislead the public has serious consequences. NOAA documents that climate change impacts are intensifying: extreme heat events, changing precipitation patterns, sea level rise, and ecosystem disruption.

Misinformation Tactic Identified

Pattern: This represents a classic "both sides" disinformation strategy - presenting fringe interpretations of legitimate science to create false balance and delay climate action. The study itself contains no controversy among climate scientists. The "controversy" was manufactured by selectively reporting findings while omitting critical context about timescales and current warming trends.

The IPCC has stated with "high confidence" that human influence is the dominant cause of observed warming since the mid-20th century. The atmospheric CO2 concentration has increased from pre-industrial levels of 280 ppm to over 420 ppm in 2025 - a level not seen in at least 3 million years.

Bottom Line

MISLEADING

The claim that a "new ice age study" contradicts climate science is fundamentally misleading. The research is legitimate paleoclimate science examining orbital cycles that operate over tens of thousands of years - timescales completely irrelevant to current climate policy. NASA and NOAA data confirm global temperatures are rising rapidly, not cooling. This represents deliberate context stripping designed to create confusion and downplay the immediate threat of anthropogenic climate change.
