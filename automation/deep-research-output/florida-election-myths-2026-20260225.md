## Executive Summary

ClickOrlando published a comprehensive debunking of 39 Florida election myths on February 19, 2026 — five days before Trump used his 2026 State of the Union address to tell 30+ million viewers that election "cheating is rampant." The timing is not coincidental: election misinformation is most virulent in the year before high-stakes midterms, and the 2026 cycle — in which Florida's governor's mansion, a Senate seat, and all 28 U.S. House seats are on the ballot — has become fertile ground for false narratives. The myths cluster into five categories: voting machine fraud (claims that machines are internet-connected, hackable, or rigged), mail-in ballot fraud (claims that mass fraud occurs via absentee voting), noncitizen voting (claims that millions of "illegal aliens" tip elections), voter roll manipulation (claims of partisan purges targeting Democrats), and procedural myths (claims about ID requirements, language access, ballot blanks, and campaign clothing at polls). [1][2][3]

Every major myth recycled in the 2026 cycle collapses under data scrutiny. The Heritage Foundation — a conservative think tank that actively maintains an election fraud database and argues fraud is a problem — has documented only 85 cases of alleged noncitizen voting in a 21-year period (2002–2023) during which approximately 2 billion federal ballots were cast. [4] The Brennan Center for Justice found just 30 suspected noncitizen votes out of 23.5 million cast across 42 high-immigration jurisdictions in 2016, a rate of 0.0001 percent. [5] CISA, the federal cybersecurity agency, stated after the 2024 election that "our election infrastructure has never been more secure" and reported "no evidence of any malicious activity that had a material impact on election infrastructure security." [6] Florida's own system uses paper ballots statewide, requires photo ID, and conducts post-election audits — a layered security architecture that election security experts describe as robust. [7]

The persistence of election myths is not accidental. Researchers at the Brennan Center and political analysts at multiple institutions have documented a deliberate strategy: by maintaining a fog of doubt around election integrity, political actors create a pre-emptive framework for contesting unfavorable results. Trump's 2026 SOTU demand for a "war on fraud" led by Vice President Vance, combined with his administration's demand that 47 states hand over complete voter registration lists, represents a structural escalation — using official government power to institutionalize and amplify claims that his own Justice Department, his first-term CISA director, and his first-term Attorney General William Barr all described as false. [8][9][10]

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin

The modern iteration of Florida-specific election myths can be traced to three overlapping sources:

1. **Trump's national "Stop the Steal" narrative (2020–present):** Trump lost the 2020 election but refused to concede, promoting claims that Dominion voting machines switched votes and that mass noncitizen voting occurred. Every specific claim was litigated in court and rejected — including by Trump-appointed judges. His first-term Attorney General William Barr called the fraud narrative "bullshit" under oath. Yet by 2026, Trump was repeating the same framework from the SOTU podium.

2. **Florida-specific political dynamics:** Florida has trended sharply Republican since 2020. Republicans have swept every statewide office. Rather than acknowledging this genuine political realignment, some Democratic-aligned activists began promoting countervailing myths — chiefly that the Florida Department of State was "purging Democrats" from voter rolls. ClickOrlando's debunking addresses both sides: the "stolen election" myths from the right and the "partisan purge" myths that emerged from the left.

3. **Heritage Foundation framing:** The Heritage Foundation maintains an "Election Fraud Database" explicitly designed to argue fraud is widespread. Ironically, its own data demonstrates the opposite: researchers analyzing Heritage's database have found 85 alleged noncitizen voting cases over 21 years, 10 cases involving undocumented immigrants since the 1980s, and just 10 total cases of in-person voter impersonation nationally. These numbers — drawn from Heritage's own curated database of cases — confirm rarity, not prevalence.

### Propagation

Each myth category follows a distinct propagation pathway:

- **Voting machine myths** spread primarily via Musk's X platform and Trump Truth Social, with initial amplification by Dinesh D'Souza's "2000 Mules" documentary (later debunked and retracted by its distributor). In February 2026, Musk pushed a debunked Dominion conspiracy theory at a campaign appearance, keeping the narrative active.
- **Noncitizen voting myths** are institutionally amplified: the SAVE America Act, passed by the House in 2025, was explicitly justified using false claims that millions of noncitizens voted in 2024. Trump cited these claims in his SOTU.
- **Voter roll myths** spread via viral TikTok and Instagram posts in Florida, claiming that checking your registration status would reveal Democratic "purges." Florida election supervisors spent significant resources responding to these.
- **Procedural myths** (wearing campaign clothing, leaving ballot lines blank, language requirements) spread via peer-to-peer WhatsApp and text message chains — harder to trace and debunk.

### Why It Spreads

Election myths have unusually high persistence for several structural reasons:

1. **Confirmation bias:** Voters who believe elections are stolen interpret normal processes (voter roll maintenance, mail ballot rejection for signature mismatch) as evidence of fraud.
2. **Complexity gap:** Election administration is genuinely complicated. The distinction between "inactive" and "removed" voters, or between "mail ballot requested" and "mail ballot returned," requires civic literacy that most voters lack.
3. **Institutional distrust:** Post-COVID institutional distrust accelerated; a large minority of voters express generic distrust of elections independent of specific claims.
4. **Asymmetric debunking:** A myth spreads in one tweet; the debunking requires a 3,000-word article. ClickOrlando's 39-myth piece is a direct attempt to close this gap.

### Evidence

The evidentiary picture is consistent across every independent analysis:

- **Voter fraud rates:** DOJ analysis of 2002 and 2004 federal elections found 0.00000013 percent of ballots fraudulent. The Brennan Center found impersonation fraud rates of 0.00004–0.0009 percent.
- **Mail ballot fraud:** An AP review found fewer than 475 potential fraud instances out of 25+ million votes in 2020 election contexts. A Colorado/Oregon/Washington analysis found 372 possible cases out of 14.6 million mail votes (0.0025 percent).
- **Noncitizen voting:** Heritage Foundation's own database: 85 alleged cases 2002–2023 / ~2 billion ballots = 0.000004 percent.
- **Florida machines:** Florida uses paper ballots statewide. Voting machines are not connected to the internet. Results are transmitted via secure, dedicated connections only when ready.
- **Voter roll "purge":** A News 6/WJXT analysis found 1.3 million "inactive" voters in Florida as of January 2026 — but inactive does not mean removed. Removing an inactive designation requires only contacting the county elections office.

**Significance for myth report:** The SAVE program — the flagship tool of the Trump administration's noncitizen voter narrative — has now run 46 million voters through its system and found confirmed noncitizen voting cases numbering in the dozens per state, spanning decades. Its most significant documented output has been the disenfranchisement of eligible U.S. citizens.

## Evidence Deep-Dive (Pass B)

### B1. Brennan Center — "Noncitizen Voting is Vanishingly Rare" (2025 Update)

**Brennan Center for Justice — "Noncitizen Voting is Vanishingly Rare"**
URL: https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/noncitizen-voting-vanishingly-rare

The Brennan Center's most recent synthesis of noncitizen voting research synthesizes findings from multiple state audits and federal analyses to conclude noncitizen voting remains "vanishingly rare." The report notes:
- Noncitizen voting is illegal "four times over" — federal law bars noncitizens from registering; state laws bar it; false registration is a separate federal crime; and lying on immigration forms forfeits citizenship eligibility.
- The structural incentives against noncitizen voting are overwhelming: a noncitizen who votes risks deportation, permanent bar from citizenship, and criminal prosecution — for a single vote that has essentially zero probability of affecting an election outcome.
- Academic research repeatedly finds that noncitizens vote at far lower rates than citizens even where they are legally permitted to vote in local elections.

### B2. ERIC System — Cross-State Voter Roll Accuracy Data

**ERIC, Inc. — Statistics (official ERIC data)**
URL: https://ericstates.org/statistics/

Since 2013, the Electronic Registration Information Center has helped member states maintain accurate voter rolls through cross-state data sharing. Key cumulative statistics:
- 13.5 million people identified who moved across state lines (allowing states to update registrations)
- 1.3 million in-state duplicate registrations identified
- 640,000 dead voters removed
- Nearly 29 million in-state voters whose information had changed, updated

ERIC's Membership Agreement explicitly prohibits submitting known noncitizen data, and member states filter noncitizens from outreach lists before sending materials. Critically, ERIC's informational mailings include a specific warning that only U.S. citizens may register — a designed safeguard against inadvertent registration. The system's purpose is voter roll *accuracy*, not fraud suppression — and its data shows the primary sources of roll inaccuracy are interstate moves, duplicate registrations, and deaths, not noncitizen voting.

**Note:** Florida withdrew from ERIC in 2023 alongside other Republican-led states, citing concerns about the organization. Florida now uses a separate state-managed process and the DHS SAVE agreement for roll maintenance.

### B3. Center for Election Innovation & Research — Noncitizen Claims Review (2025)

**Center for Election Innovation & Research — "Update: Review of Claims of Noncitizen Registrants and Voters" (2025)**
URL: https://electioninnovation.org/research/noncitizen-analysis-update/

CEIR's 2025 review of sweeping noncitizen voter claims found that when large-number claims are "subject to scrutiny and properly investigated, the number of alleged instances falls drastically." The research documents a consistent pattern: initial headlines claim thousands or millions of noncitizen voters; upon investigation by actual election officials using verified data, the confirmed numbers shrink to dozens or fewer, and many of those turn out to be legal immigrants who registered in error believing they were eligible. The pattern holds across every state that conducted a thorough audit.

### B4. Academic Study — Digital Voter Suppression Impact (PNAS, 2025)

**Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences — "Targeted digital voter suppression efforts likely decrease voter turnout" (2025)**
URL: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2519944123

A peer-reviewed study in PNAS provides the first systematic empirical documentation of the turnout effects of election disinformation. Key findings:
- Individuals exposed to digital voter suppression content were statistically less likely to vote.
- The suppression effect disproportionately targeted non-white voters in racial minority counties of battleground states.
- Suppression content did not need to explicitly tell voters not to vote — content designed to undermine confidence in election integrity was sufficient to depress turnout.
- These findings have direct bearing on Florida 2026: the persistent myth ecosystem described in this report is not merely epistemically harmful — it is functionally suppressive, particularly for Black, Hispanic, and immigrant-community voters in Florida's competitive districts.

### B5. Florida 2024 Post-Election Audit — Official Report

### C4. OSCE International Observer Assessment — 2024 U.S. Elections

URL: https://www.osce.org/odihr/elections/usa/580111

- The elections were "organized efficiently and professionally" with Election Day managed "professionally in polling stations visited."

- The mission issued 31 formal recommendations, focused on the administration challenges raised by misinformation campaigns, not on fraud.

The OSCE assessment directly contradicts the claim that U.S. elections are characterized by rampant cheating: independent international observers with experience monitoring elections across 57 countries rated the 2024 U.S. election as professionally conducted.

### C5. CISA Dismantlement — Brennan Center Analysis

**Brennan Center for Justice — "How the Federal Government Is Undermining Election Security" (2026)**
URL: https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/how-federal-government-undermining-election-security

The Brennan Center's 2026 analysis documents the structural paradox: the Trump administration simultaneously claims elections are riddled with fraud while dismantling the federal infrastructure responsible for detecting and documenting actual election security threats:
- CISA's election security activities paused; Election Day situation room not stood up for the first time in years during November 2025 elections.
- The Election Infrastructure Information Sharing and Analysis Center (EI-ISAC) had its funding halted — the system that alerts state officials of cross-state threats.
- Chris Krebs, who as CISA director certified 2020 as secure, had his security clearance revoked and was targeted by a DOJ probe after Trump's 2025 executive order.
- The effect: states are left to manage election security individually, without the coordinated federal support that documented the absence of fraud in 2020 and 2024. This creates the very conditions in which fraud claims become harder to systematically rebut.
