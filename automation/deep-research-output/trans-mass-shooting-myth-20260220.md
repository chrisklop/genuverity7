## Executive Summary

The claim that transgender people commit mass shootings at disproportionate rates is demonstrably false and represents one of the most persistent and harmful pieces of misinformation in contemporary American discourse. [1][2][3] According to the Violence Prevention Project at Hamline University, of 201 mass shootings recorded in the United States between 1966 and 2025, **196 were committed by cisgender men (97.5%), 4 by cisgender women (2%), and only 1 (0.5%) by a transgender person** — the March 2023 Nashville Covenant School shooting. [4][5] The Gun Violence Archive, using a broader definition, counted 5,748 mass shootings between January 2013 and September 2025, with only **5 confirmed transgender shooters (0.09%)** and up to 8 if unconfirmed cases are included. [6][7]

For comparison, approximately **0.8-1.3% of the U.S. adult population identifies as transgender**, meaning transgender individuals are statistically **underrepresented** among mass shooters, not overrepresented. [8][9] As Professor Mia Bloom of Georgia State University stated, "When you're looking at the average violence across the community, disproportionately, you know it's white, straight men." [10] Dr. James Densley, co-founder of the Violence Prevention Project, noted the "long and ugly history" of associating entire marginalized groups with mass violence based on statistically negligible cases, emphasizing that such scapegoating "said more about the politics of the moment than about any actual causal relationship." [11]

The real story is one of victimization, not perpetration. Since 2013, the Human Rights Campaign has documented **399 transgender and gender-nonconforming people killed in the United States**, with 70% murdered by firearms and 84.4% being people of color — predominantly Black trans women. [12][13] Trans people are **four times more likely to be victims of crime** than cisgender people. [14] Yet this epidemic of violence against transgender people has been weaponized into false narratives that paint them as threats, creating a feedback loop where misinformation justifies legislative attacks (500+ anti-trans bills introduced in 2023), which in turn increase vulnerability to violence. [15][16]

The pattern of misinformation is systematic: immediately following mass shootings, false claims emerge on platforms like 4chan, are amplified by right-wing influencers and politicians before facts are established, and persist even after being debunked. [17][18] The February 2026 Tumbler Ridge shooting in Canada reignited these false claims, with Elon Musk, Russia Today, and Canadian politician Tara Armstrong declaring an "epidemic of transgender violence" despite data showing no such pattern. [19][20] As Kasari Govender, British Columbia's human rights commissioner, responded: "Using this horrific incident to conflate trans identities with violent tendencies is incorrect, irresponsible and frankly dangerous." [21]

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin: How the Myth Took Root

While isolated instances of misattribution occurred earlier, the modern false narrative that transgender people disproportionately commit mass shootings crystallized around two pivotal events:

**1. Uvalde, Texas (May 24, 2022)** — The Template for Misinformation

Within hours of the Robb Elementary School shooting that killed 19 children and 2 teachers, 4chan users posted photos of an unrelated transgender woman ("Sam") with false claims she was the shooter. [22][23] Despite authorities having already identified the shooter as Salvador Ramos, an 18-year-old male, the lie spread rapidly:

- Rep. Paul Gosar (R-AZ) tweeted the shooter was a "transexual leftist illegal alien named Salvatore Ramos" — wrong on all counts. [24]
- Candace Owens told her 3 million Twitter followers she'd seen "cross-dressing" photos of the suspect. [25]
- Alex Jones echoed the false claim on Infowars. [26]
- The innocent trans woman whose photos were stolen faced death threats and harassment for days. [27]

Even 4chan users mocked Gosar for falling for "obvious click bait," with one writing: "It was a lie, spread by this board." [28] Yet the template was established: flood the information space with false trans attribution immediately after shootings, before facts emerge, knowing corrections never fully catch up to the initial viral spread.

**2. Nashville, Tennessee (March 27, 2023)** — The "Manifesto" That Wasn't

When Aiden Hale, a 28-year-old transgender man, killed three children and three adults at The Covenant School before being killed by police, it became the first mass shooting in the Violence Prevention Project's database with a confirmed transgender perpetrator. [29][30] The New York Times and police initially described Hale as having a "manifesto," a characterization that defined coverage for two years. [31]

In reality, as the Nashville Metro Police Department's April 2025 final report concluded: "**In this case, a manifesto didn't exist.**" [32] Hale left no single document explaining motives, but rather 16 notebooks with over 1,000 pages of "rambling" writings expressing suicidal ideation, desire for notoriety, and planning. Investigators determined Hale was motivated by wanting to die in a place he had happy memories (he attended the school as a child) and to achieve fame. The MNPD explicitly stated the attack was **not motivated by race, not motivated by a grudge, and targeted Christians because Hale believed "their faith would make them meek and afraid,"** ensuring instant media attention. [33][34]

Yet conservative media and politicians seized on this single case to construct a pattern where none existed, amplifying it into claims of a "trans terrorism" epidemic.

### Propagation: The Misinformation Machine

The false narrative spreads through a coordinated ecosystem:

**Phase 1: Immediate Misattribution (Hours 0-24)**

- Anonymous users on 4chan, 8kun, and extremist forums post photos of random trans people with false shooter claims
- Before authorities release confirmed information, baseless speculation floods social media
- As Joe Ondrak of Logically AI explained: "Social media trolls pump out disinformation before a shooter has been identified because they know that our news cycle moves so quickly now that a lot of the attention will just evaporate by the time the truth surfaces." [35]

**Phase 2: Amplification by Influencers (Hours 12-72)**

- Right-wing accounts with millions of followers (Libs of TikTok, Chaya Raichik, Donald Trump Jr.) share unverified claims
- Politicians (Paul Gosar, Ronny Jackson) legitimize misinformation through official channels
- Foreign propaganda outlets (Russia Today) amplify divisive narratives
- Tech CEOs (Elon Musk) use platform ownership to boost false claims [36][37]

**Phase 3: Viral Charts & Memes (Days-Weeks)**

- Fabricated data visualizations claiming trans people are "highest per capita" mass shooters circulate
- These charts include shooters whose trans identity was never confirmed (Colorado Springs Club Q shooter Anderson Lee Aldrich) or was demonstrably false [38][39]
- Charts ignore base rates: with only 1-2 trans-identified shooters out of thousands of incidents, any non-zero number appears inflated when calculated "per capita" for a 1% population
- Gun Violence Archive and other databases confirm they never published data supporting viral charts [40]

**Phase 4: Legislative & Policy Weaponization (Months-Years)**

- Following Nashville shooting, U.S. Department of Justice considered policies to **ban transgender people from owning guns** based on false premise they're more prone to mass shootings [41]
- 500+ anti-trans bills introduced in 2023 alone, with many citing "safety concerns" rooted in violence misinformation [42]
- As University of Buffalo professor Yotam Ophir warned: "If you persuade conservatives that LGBTQ people are inherently flawed, that they are violent, that they are a risk to society, then any legislation against them will be justified." [43]

### Why It Spreads: The Perfect Storm

**1. Confirmation Bias in a Polarized Era**

The claim resonates with audiences already primed by years of anti-trans rhetoric framing trans people as "dangerous" or "mentally ill." It provides seemingly empirical justification for pre-existing prejudice.

**2. Novelty Bias & Availability Heuristic**

Because trans-identified mass shooters are so rare, each case feels more memorable than the 99%+ committed by cisgender men. Dr. Densley calls this "base rate neglect" — "even a couple of incidents in a short window can make something feel like a pattern even when it's not." [44]

**3. Deflection from Gun Control Debate**

As analysts noted, false trans claims serve dual purposes: "to push anti-trans hatred and to divert debate about the shooting away from issues like gun-control legislation." [45] Framing mass shootings as a "trans issue" redirects from the overwhelming pattern of cisgender male violence and access to firearms.

**4. Historical Scapegoating Playbook**

The tactic of blaming marginalized groups for societal violence has been used against Jewish people, immigrants, Muslims, and other minorities throughout history. As Densley observed: "Associating an entire marginalized group with mass violence based on a statistically negligible number of cases has a long and ugly history. In each case, the perceived pattern said more about the politics of the moment than about any actual causal relationship." [46]

**5. Algorithm-Driven Amplification**

Inflammatory, emotion-laden false claims generate more engagement than corrections, leading social media algorithms to preferentially distribute misinformation. Posts falsely blaming trans people for shootings remain up even after debunking, continuing to spread. [47]

**Statistical Conclusion**

If transgender people committed mass shootings at the same rate as their population share, we would expect:
- Violence Prevention Project: ~2 trans shooters out of 201 (1%)

**Actual numbers:**
- Violence Prevention Project: 1 (0.5% — underrepresented by 50%)
- Gun Violence Archive: 5 (0.09% — underrepresented by 91%)

As Ohio State University professor Laura Dugan stated: "The risk of transgender people committing shootings is just not a concern, and transgender people are actually **underrepresented** as mass shooters." [57]

**The Victimization Reality**

While trans people account for <1% of mass shooters, they are vastly overrepresented as victims:

- **399+ trans/gender-nonconforming people killed since 2013** (Human Rights Campaign tracking) [58]

As one researcher summarized: "Trans people are substantially **more likely to be victims than perpetrators** of violence." [64]

Every moral panic centers on a **"folk devil"** — a scapegoat serving as a pawn for the powerful. Folk devils usually belong to communities already marginalized because of race, ethnicity, class, sexuality, nationality, or religion. [94][95]

The storm around trans people bears all the hallmarks of a moral panic. Trans people are blamed for a number of — often contradictory — harms, including corrupting children, threatening free speech, violence against women, and both dismantling and reinforcing problematic gender norms. [96]

### How Social Media Algorithms Amplify the False Narrative

Research in 2025 confirms that social media algorithms driven by engagement metrics amplify emotionally charged and identity-based misinformation, including false claims about transgender people:

**Echo chambers & algorithmic amplification:**

In the digital age, algorithms curate content that aligns with users' interests and preferences, further entrenching their views. Content can be amplified through engagement-focused algorithms, enabling conspiracy theories to spread — especially if the content is **emotionally charged or polarizing**. [122][123]

The phenomenon of **selective exposure** — where users actively choose content that confirms pre-existing beliefs while disregarding opposing viewpoints — lies at the core of echo chambers. Confirmation bias contributes to this dynamic and creates a feedback loop that is difficult to escape. These biases are further amplified by **algorithmic prioritization based on user engagement metrics**. [124]

**Amplification of false claims:**

Algorithms amplify polarization by promoting **sensational or emotionally charged content that drives engagement**, often favoring divisive viewpoints that push users toward more extreme positions. This affects democracy by shaping public discourse, spreading misinformation, and eroding trust in institutions. [125]

As one researcher noted: **"The more you are exposed to certain information, the more you're going to believe it's true, even if it's inaccurate."** [126]

**Identity-driven disinformation:**

Researchers define disinformation as "an adversarial campaign that weaponizes multiple rhetorical strategies and forms of knowing — including not only falsehoods but also truths, half-truths, and value-laden judgments — to **exploit and amplify identity-driven controversies**." [127]

The first phase of disinformation dissemination is **"seeding,"** in which actors strategically insert misleading narratives by hiding the source, decontextualizing information, and camouflaging them as legitimate conversations in trusted information sources. [128]

**The volume problem:**

The sheer volume of online information can result in **"false confirmation"** — a phenomenon where a piece of information seems to be reported by multiple sources when there is actually only one. Multiple outlets reporting a story while drawing on the same original source artificially amplifies stories that might have little actual support, making individuals more likely to believe them through **communal reinforcement**. [129]

**AI echo chambers (2025):**

AI-driven recommendation systems have significantly influenced digital information consumption, leading to the formation of **"AI echo chambers"** that reinforce users' preexisting beliefs by continuously feeding them similar content. A systematic review synthesizing a decade of peer-reviewed research (2015–2025) found strong growth in interest after 2018, aligning with heightened public concern over misinformation and polarization. [130][131]

**Proposed solutions:**

Strategies to mitigate these effects include algorithmic transparency, diverse content exposure, and user-driven interactions as potential methods for breaking echo chambers. Media literacy teaches users to critically evaluate information and recognize echo chambers, empowering them to resist algorithmic biases. [132]

### Cognitive Biases That Fuel the False Narrative

Two well-studied psychological phenomena explain why people dramatically overestimate the frequency of transgender mass shooters:

**Base Rate Neglect:**

Base rate neglect is a cognitive bias that occurs when individuals ignore or underweight the **base rate** of an event in favor of more specific, but potentially less reliable, information. This leads to an overemphasis on anecdotal evidence while ignoring statistical realities. [152][153]

When a rare event (like a mass shooting) is associated with a minority group (like transgender people), people tend to ignore the actual base rate (trans people commit <0.1% of mass shootings, while comprising ~1% of the population) and instead focus on the dramatic individual cases.

**The Availability Heuristic:**

The availability heuristic refers to the tendency to judge the likelihood of an event based on how easily examples come to mind. When vivid or dramatic instances are readily available in memory, they can disproportionately influence perception of reality — leading people to overestimate the frequency or severity of certain events. [154][155]

**How these biases interact:**

When under pressure, people resort to cognitive shortcuts like the availability heuristic and the representativeness heuristic — **factoring in only current and vivid information, and ignoring the actual probability** of something happening (i.e., the base rate). [156]

Additionally, the availability heuristic can lead to an overestimation of frequency, which leads to neglect of the base rate and ultimately biased judgment. **Confirmation bias** can perpetuate base rate neglect by causing individuals to selectively focus on information that supports their initial judgment, rather than considering base rate information that may contradict it. [157]

**Application to group perception:**

If a juror hears about a particularly heinous crime committed by an individual from a specific demographic group, they may unconsciously allow that case to shape their perceptions of all individuals from that group. This can perpetuate stereotypes and biases, affecting fairness and equity. [158]

This same mechanism applies to transgender people: **high-profile, media-covered incidents** involving transgender perpetrators trigger the availability heuristic, causing people to dramatically overestimate how frequently members of that group commit violent acts, despite the actual statistical base rate being very low. [159]

As Dr. James Densley explained, this is **"base rate neglect"** — **"even a couple of incidents in a short window can make something feel like a pattern even when it's not."** [160]

**Combating these biases:**

Several strategies can help mitigate base rate neglect, including improving cognitive awareness, debiasing decision-making, and enhancing critical thinking. Education plays a pivotal role by fostering critical thinking skills and promoting statistical literacy. By equipping individuals with the tools to analyze data effectively, educational institutions can help combat cognitive biases that lead to flawed decision-making. [161]
