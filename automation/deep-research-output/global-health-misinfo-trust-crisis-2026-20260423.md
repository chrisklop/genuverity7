## Executive Summary

A landmark survey published April 22, 2026 by Edelman has laid bare the scale of the global health misinformation crisis: seven in ten people worldwide now believe at least one of six widely-debunked health claims to be true. [1] The 2026 Edelman Trust Barometer Special Report on Health, drawing on responses from more than 16,000 people across 16 countries, found that public confidence in finding reliable health information has fallen 10 percentage points in a single year to just 51%. [1] Richard Edelman, CEO of Edelman, called the findings "quite a stunning set of facts," noting that health misinformation skeptics are "everybody, not just fringe groups." [1]

The crisis is not merely statistical. In the United States, it has manifested as the worst measles resurgence in three decades: 1,748 confirmed cases by April 16, 2026, tracking toward a total that would surpass all of 2025's 2,288 cases. [5] The U.S. now risks losing its measles elimination status, achieved in 2000. [6] At the same time, Elon Musk amplified a claim — viewed 60 million times — that COVID-19 vaccines killed tens of thousands of people in Germany, a claim debunked by FactCheck.org, epidemiologists, and the German government. [7] Meanwhile, HHS Secretary RFK Jr. promoted vitamin A as a measles treatment, prompting children to be hospitalized with vitamin A toxicity. [9]

These are not isolated incidents. They are symptoms of what researchers describe as an "information ecosystem collapse" — one in which the sheer abundance of health content, not its scarcity, has made it impossible for the average person to determine who to trust. [1] The Edelman data shows that education level does not protect against health misinformation: 69% of university graduates hold at least one debunked belief, virtually identical to the 70% of those without degrees. [1] The collapse is universal.

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin

The global health misinformation trust crisis has no single origin. It is the product of converging forces: the dismantling of CDC/FDA credibility under the DOGE era, the elevation of anti-vaccine advocates to cabinet-level positions (RFK Jr.), the platform amplification of anti-scientific content by billionaire platform owners (Elon Musk on X), and the algorithmically rewarded spread of health fear content on TikTok and YouTube.

The measles misinformation strand traces to RFK Jr.'s promotion of vitamin A as a measles substitute beginning in 2025. The COVID vaccine misinformation strand traces to a March 19, 2026 German parliamentary hearing where Dr. Helmut Sterz testified that COVID vaccines killed 20,000–60,000 people in Germany — a figure he derived by arbitrarily multiplying official death reports by 30 to account for alleged underreporting. [7]

### Propagation

- **April 12, 2026:** Elon Musk reshared Peter Imanuelsen's post about Sterz's testimony, adding personal commentary. Musk's post accumulated nearly 60 million views; Imanuelsen's 64 million. [7]
- **Ongoing 2025–2026:** RFK Jr. repeatedly promoted vitamin A on television and in HHS communications. During the West Texas measles outbreak, the U.S. government sent extra doses of vitamin A to affected communities. Several children were then hospitalized with vitamin A toxicity. [9]
- **April 22, 2026:** RFK Jr. testified before Senate Finance and HELP committees, where Sen. Ben Ray Luján accused him of "pushing vaccine misinformation." [10]
- The Edelman survey captured the cumulative effect: trust in media to accurately cover health topics stood at 46% — 11 points below pre-COVID levels. [1]

### Why It Spread

1. **Institutional authority vacuum:** With the CDC, FDA, and NIH perceived as politicized under RFK Jr.'s HHS leadership, Americans have lost their default credibility anchor for health information.
2. **Billionaire amplification:** Musk's 200M+ follower account functions as a misinformation amplifier. A single post can reach more people than any public health campaign.
3. **Algorithm reward structure:** Health fear content consistently outperforms accurate health content on engagement metrics across TikTok, YouTube, and Facebook.
4. **Emotional resonance:** Vaccine skepticism and "natural" cures satisfy psychological needs (autonomy, distrust of authority, fear of government control) that accurate information struggles to address.
5. **Cross-partisan appeal:** The Edelman data shows 78% of right-leaning and 64% of left-leaning people hold at least one false health belief — it is not a partisan phenomenon.

### Evidence

- **Germany COVID vaccine claim:** Paul Ehrlich Institute (PEI) recorded 2,133 death reports following Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination. PEI assessed only 28 as having "possible or probable" causal relationship. Over 138 million doses were administered. Dr. Sterz arbitrarily multiplied 2,133 by 30. [7] German Health Minister Karl Lauterbach described the claims as false. [7]
- **Vitamin A/measles:** U.S. MMR vaccine is 97% effective at preventing measles. Vitamin A does not prevent measles and does not function as an antiviral. High-dose vitamin A supplementation is indicated only for malnourished children in low-income countries where vitamin A deficiency is common. [9] [8] In the U.S., vitamin A deficiency is rare; high-dose supplementation caused toxicity in American children. [9]
- **Edelman 6 myths:** Surveyed 16,000 people; 70% believed at least one of: animal protein healthier (32%), fluoride harmful (32%), childhood vaccination risks outweigh benefits (31%), raw milk healthier than pasteurized (28%), acetaminophen during pregnancy causes autism (25%), vaccines used for population control (25%). [1]

## Claim vs Reality

| Claim | Reality | Verdict |
|-------|---------|---------|
| COVID-19 vaccines killed 20,000–60,000 people in Germany (Musk/Sterz claim) | PEI recorded 2,133 death *reports* (not confirmed deaths); only 28 assessed as possibly/probably vaccine-related out of 138M doses. The 60,000 figure was obtained by arbitrarily multiplying 2,133 by 30. | **False** |
| Vitamin A can treat or prevent measles (RFK Jr.) | Vitamin A does not prevent measles and is not an antiviral. It reduces mortality only in malnourished children in low-income settings. High-dose supplementation caused hospitalization for toxicity in US children. | **Misleading / Dangerous** |
| Childhood vaccination risks outweigh benefits (believed by 31% globally) | Measles vaccines (MMR) are 97% effective. The measles virus kills 1–2 per 1,000 infected in developed nations and up to 1 in 5 in malnourished populations. The main claimed risk (autism-MMR link) was a retracted fraud. | **False** |
| Fluoride in water is harmful or unhelpful (believed by 32% globally) | Community water fluoridation reduces dental cavities by 25% per CDC meta-analysis. At regulated doses (0.7 mg/L in the U.S.), no credible evidence of harm exists. | **False** |
| Raw milk is healthier than pasteurized milk (28% globally) | Raw milk carries pathogens including Salmonella, E. coli O157:H7, and Listeria. The FDA states pasteurization does not meaningfully reduce nutritional value. | **False / Dangerous** |
| Vaccines are used for population control (25% globally) | No credible evidence for this claim exists. It originates from misinterpretation of Bill Gates quotes and has been repeatedly debunked by WHO, CDC, and independent researchers. | **False** |
