## 1. Hollywood IP in Political Propaganda

### Trump Administration Copyright Pattern (2024-2026)

- Jack & Meg White (White Stripes) filed copyright complaint
- Multiple artists demanded Trump stop using their music at rallies
- NPR documented systemic pattern: "More and more artists want Trump to stop using their music"

**Second Term (2025-2026):**
- **March 2025:** White House/Border Patrol used Semisonic's "Closing Time" without authorization for deportation video
- **September 2025:** DHS used Pokémon footage in ICE arrest montage — Pokémon Company: "permission was not granted"
- **December 2025:** White House posted "Just Getting Started" video using Gladiator footage (Russell Crowe as Maximus)
- **March 2026:** "JUSTICE THE AMERICAN WAY" supercut — 15+ Hollywood films/shows/games used without permission
- Wikipedia maintains dedicated article: "Use of copyrighted works by the second Trump administration"

**Key legal note:** No copyright infringement lawsuits have been filed against the Trump *administration* (as distinct from the campaign) as of March 2026. Government speech may have different legal frameworks than campaign speech.

## 2. TikTok Platform Enforcement

### State-Affiliated Content Policies
- TikTok began publishing monthly Covert Influence Operations reports in 2024
- Dedicated transparency page: tiktok.com/transparency/en/covert-influence-operations

### Iran-Specific Enforcement
- **February 2024:** 65 Iran-backed accounts removed
- **May 2024:** Iran-based network targeting UK users identified and removed
- **October 2024:** 26,964 accounts removed from previously disrupted networks (all countries)
- **January 2025:** 3,743 accounts removed from previously disrupted networks

### Broader Enforcement Statistics (2024-2025)
- **Jan-Apr 2024:** 15 influence operations disrupted, 3,001 accounts removed
- **July-Dec 2025:** 112 million pieces of violative content removed (all categories)
- TikTok publishes EU Digital Services Act transparency reports biannually

### Iran + TikTok Paradox
- TikTok is **blocked in Iran** as part of broader internet censorship
- Yet Iranian state-affiliated content circulates globally on the platform
- This creates an asymmetry: Iran can project propaganda onto TikTok without its own citizens having access to counter-narratives

### Meta/Cross-Platform Context
- **Meta Q1 2025:** Dismantled covert influence campaigns from China, Iran, and Romania
- **Meta December 2024:** Dismantled Iranian and Hezbollah influence operations targeting Israel
- **Global Influence Ops Report:** Documented "Iranian TikTok AI Disinformation Campaign: Five Strategies Exposed"

## 4. Cross-Platform Comparison

**TikTok:**
- Monthly covert influence operation reports

- Removed tens of thousands of accounts
- Iran blocked domestically but content circulates globally

## 5. The "Commentator Effect": Third-Party Amplification

The TikTok video features a commentator (@user5626007079271) who:
- Shows both the White House video AND the Iran video
- Editorializes with apparent admiration for the Iran animation's "production quality"
- Functions as an **amplification node** — bringing state propaganda to audiences who wouldn't seek it out
- This "neutral commentary" framing makes propaganda more shareable than the original sources

### Why This Matters
- State propaganda from official channels has known bias and limited reach
- Third-party amplification strips the propaganda of its state-media branding
- Admiring the "creativity" of propaganda normalizes it as entertainment
- TikTok's algorithm rewards engagement regardless of the source's intent
