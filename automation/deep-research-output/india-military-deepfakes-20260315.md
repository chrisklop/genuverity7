## Executive Summary

Between March 9–13, 2026, a coordinated pro-Pakistan AI disinformation campaign produced and distributed at least ten deepfake videos falsely depicting senior Indian government and military officials supporting Israel against Iran. The primary delivery vehicle was @InsiderWB, a pro-Pakistan X account that has been sharing doctored media since the India-Pakistan conflict of May 2025 and is now withheld in India following a legal demand [1, 2]. The campaign exploited a geopolitically charged moment: on March 4, 2026, a US Navy submarine had torpedoed the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena near Sri Lanka — a ship that had just participated in India's International Fleet Review 2026 — killing approximately 87 crew members and triggering international accusations that India had leaked the ship's position to Israel [3].

The most consequential fabrication showed Army Chief General Upendra Dwivedi — speaking genuine footage from a Raisina Dialogue 2026 interview published March 7 — with entirely synthetic audio inserted claiming "as Israeli strategic allies, it was our duty to inform Israel of their exact location as part of our newer strategic deal" [4, 5]. India's Press Information Bureau (PIB) Fact Check unit debunked this on March 9, confirming that Dwivedi's actual remarks concerned Operation Sindoor lessons and India's deterrence posture — with no mention of Iran, Israel, or the IRIS Dena [6]. The fake audio was confirmed by Hiva Deepfake Voice Detector (score: 1/100 authenticity) and Hive Moderation (99.9% AI-generated probability) [4, 7]. Separately, deepfakes of Defence Minister Rajnath Singh, External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar, and MEA Spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal were debunked between March 3–13, all fabricated from archived genuine speeches [8, 9, 10].

The campaign is consistent with documented patterns of ISPR (Inter-Services Public Relations)-linked information operations against India. India's PIB Fact Check unit has debunked over 2,400 false items in four years, with 800+ in the past year alone [11]. The operation is part of a broader Pakistan-linked coordinated campaign that X separately identified on March 6, when the platform exposed a single Pakistani operator running 31 hacked accounts renamed "Iran War Monitor" to spread AI-generated war videos using OpenAI's Sora 2 [12]. The strategic goal appears threefold: damage India's carefully maintained neutrality in the Iran-Israel conflict, implicate India in the death of IRIS Dena crew members, and inflame domestic Hindu-Muslim tensions by portraying India's government as aligned with Israel against Muslim nations.

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin

The deepfake operation launched no later than March 9, 2026, when the Army Chief video began circulating. The original source material for the Dwivedi deepfake was a 21-minute Firstpost YouTube interview from the Raisina Dialogue 2026 (published March 7, 2026), titled "India's Army Chief Speaks on Op Sindoor, Pakistan and Future of Warfare." A 57-second segment was extracted, genuine video retained, and the entire audio track replaced with AI-synthesized speech containing fabricated claims about sharing IRIS Dena's location with Israel [4].

The Rajnath Singh deepfake was sourced from an even older clip — his November 23, 2025 speech at the Sindhi Samaj Sammelan in Delhi about Sindhi culture — with new audio falsely depicting him claiming India supported Israel's strikes on Iran [8]. The Jaishankar deepfake emerged during his parliamentary briefing on West Asia tensions, with AI voice falsely inserting claims that "India would not tolerate Muslim countries harassing Israel" and that Israel had granted $3 billion to the Afghan Taliban at India's request [9].

The @InsiderWB account has the following profile characteristics: follows only 11 accounts (predominantly Pakistan-based journalists and political leaders); has been active since at least the May 2025 India-Pakistan conflict; and has shared doctored media at high frequency during geopolitical crises. X withheld the account in India following a legal demand [1].

### Propagation Path

1. **@InsiderWB** posts deepfakes on X (March 9–13, 2026)
2. **Pakistani propaganda accounts** amplify via coordinated network
3. **WhatsApp** chains carry videos into Indian communities
4. **International pickup**: Turkish newspaper Yeni Safak cited the Dwivedi deepfake as authentic, claiming "India admitted to sharing the location of an Iranian ship with Israel" — extending the disinformation beyond South Asia [13]
5. **Indian Embassy in Oman** was forced to issue public warnings against the Army Chief deepfake to contain spread in the Gulf region [14]

### Why It Spread

Three factors amplified the campaign beyond typical deepfake reach:

**Geopolitical plausibility gap:** India had just hosted IRIS Dena in its International Fleet Review. The ship was sunk March 4 by a US submarine. No explanation had been given for how the submarine located it. The deepfakes filled this explanatory vacuum with a false but emotionally coherent narrative.

**Quality of fabrication:** The Dwivedi deepfake used genuine footage from a real, newsworthy interview (Raisina Dialogue) so the visuals were authentic. Only the audio was synthetic — a technique that exploits the fact that most casual viewers process video authority through recognizable faces in recognizable contexts.

**IRIS Dena's Indian connection:** The ship had participated in Indian Navy exercises at Visakhapatnam (February 15–25, 2026), creating a factual basis for the conspiratorial inference that India had intelligence on its movements.

### Evidence of State Linkage

India's government, including the PIB and Ministry of External Affairs, characterized the campaign as coordinated Pakistani disinformation linked to ISPR (the media wing of Pakistan's Inter-Services Intelligence). ISPR operates a documented 3-wing structure: Management/Public Affairs, Production/Content Creation, and Monitoring/Analysis. Reports indicate ISPR maintains approximately 4,000 information warfare specialists trained through ISI-run programs [15]. The timing of the deepfake surge (coinciding with the IRIS Dena sinking and India's declared neutrality) aligns with ISPR's documented strategy of exploiting India-Israel-Muslim country tensions to inflame domestic communal divides [15, 16].

### B2. Rajnath Singh Deepfake — Actual Original Quote vs. Fabrication

The authentic Rajnath Singh speech at the Sindhi Samaj Sammelan (November 23, 2025) contained no geopolitical content. His actual statement, confirmed by FACTLY's source review, concerned cultural heritage: **"Today, the land of Sindh may not be a part of India, but civilisationally, Sindh will always be a part of India."** The disinformation operation excised this cultural address entirely and replaced the audio with fabricated war rhetoric — exploiting the visual authority of a formal government event.

### B3. Hiya Voice Detector — Technical Meaning of "1/100"

The Hiya Deepfake Voice Detector returns an **authenticity score from 0–100**, where 100 = fully authentic human voice and 0 = completely AI-synthesized. A score of **1/100** (as returned for the Dwivedi deepfake) is effectively the floor of the scale — indicating maximum confidence of AI generation with minimal residual authentic voice characteristics.
