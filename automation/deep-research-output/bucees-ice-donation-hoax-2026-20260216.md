## Executive Summary

In late January 2026, a completely fabricated claim spread across social media alleging that Buc-ee's CEO Arch "Beaver" Aplin III donated $1 million to U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). [1] The hoax originated from Instagram accounts @floridafoos and @mexicanworldstarfoo, which posted the claim without any sources, citations, or evidence. [2] One viral Instagram post garnered more than 283,000 likes, while another accumulated over 90,000 likes, sparking widespread backlash and calls for boycotts of the beloved Texas-based travel center chain. [3]

Buc-ee's General Counsel Jeff Nadalo swiftly and categorically denied the claim: "The circulating rumors about Beaver donating to ICE are entirely false. Neither the company nor anyone associated with Buc-ee's has made any donations to ICE." [4] An exhaustive search of public records, FEC filings, and government databases revealed no evidence whatsoever supporting the allegation. [5]

The hoax emerged during a period of intense anti-ICE activism in early 2026, following the fatal shootings of Renée Nicole Macklin Good (January 7) and Alex Pretti (January 24) by federal agents in Minneapolis. [6] This sparked the "National Shutdown" and "ICE OUT" strikes on January 30–31, 2026, with hundreds of demonstrations nationwide and boycott campaigns targeting companies with actual ICE contracts such as Palantir ($30 million for ImmigrationOS), Microsoft ($20 million), and Amazon (via AWS cloud infrastructure). [7][8] In this politically charged environment, the Buc-ee's hoax weaponized legitimate activism to spread a demonstrable lie.

The claim confused a real $1 million donation—which Aplin presented on NBC's *Today Show* in August 2025 to the Community Foundation of the Texas Hill Country for flood relief following the devastating July 4, 2025 floods that killed over 130 people—with an entirely fictitious ICE donation. [9][10] While Aplin has a documented history of political donations to Texas Republicans including Gov. Greg Abbott ($575,000), Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick ($250,000), and Attorney General Ken Paxton, there are zero records of any ICE donations. [11][12]

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin: The Birth of a Hoax

**Patient Zero Identified:**
The hoax originated from two Instagram accounts: **@floridafoos** and **@mexicanworldstarfoo**. These accounts posted claims that Arch "Beaver" Aplin III "reportedly donated $1 million to support ICE operations" without providing any footnote, source, link, or evidence for the allegation. [13] The accounts simply fabricated the claim and called for a boycott of Buc-ee's.

**Why These Accounts?**
@floridafoos describes itself as the "#1 Source For News, Music, & Viral Clips" and directs followers to its sister account @mexicanworldstarfoo. [14] Despite positioning themselves as news sources, neither account cited any documentation, public records, or credible reporting to substantiate the ICE donation claim.

**Timeline:**
- **January 2026:** Initial posts appear on @floridafoos and @mexicanworldstarfoo making the ICE donation claim
- **Late January 2026:** Posts go viral across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and X (Twitter)
- **Early February 2026:** Buc-ee's issues official denial through General Counsel Jeff Nadalo
- **February 2–4, 2026:** Media outlets including Fox 26 Houston, KNUE, and Yahoo News publish fact-checks debunking the hoax

### Propagation: How the Hoax Spread

**Engagement Metrics:**
- **Instagram Post #1:** 283,000+ likes, nearly 13,000 comments [15]
- **Instagram Post #2:** 90,000+ likes [16]
- **Facebook Posts:** Several posts approached nearly 1,000 likes each by February 4 [17]

**Multi-Platform Amplification:**
The hoax didn't stay on Instagram. It spread to:
- **Facebook:** Multiple shares and posts with hundreds of likes
- **TikTok:** Video content amplifying the boycott call
- **X (Twitter):** Trending topic with searches for "Buc-ee's ICE donations" and "Buc-ee's CEO political donations" [18]

**Velocity Analysis:**
The hoax achieved viral velocity within days, not weeks. The combination of:
1. A polarizing political issue (ICE enforcement)
2. A culturally beloved brand (Buc-ee's)
3. A specific, memorable dollar amount ($1 million)
4. A climate of heightened anti-ICE activism

...created perfect conditions for rapid spread before fact-checking could catch up.

### Why It Spread: The Perfect Storm

**1. Political Context — National Anti-ICE Uprising**

The hoax emerged during the most intense period of anti-ICE activism in recent U.S. history:

- **January 7, 2026:** ICE agent Jonathan Ross fatally shot Renée Nicole Macklin Good, a 37-year-old American woman, in Minneapolis [19]
- **January 24, 2026:** Alex Pretti was shot multiple times and killed by Customs and Border Protection agents [20]
- **January 30–31, 2026:** Massive "National Shutdown" and "ICE OUT" strikes with hundreds of demonstrations nationwide; activists called for "no school, no work, no shopping" to protest Trump administration immigration policies [21][22]
- **January 31, 2026:** Over 300 "ICE Out of Everywhere" protests organized by coalitions including the 50501 movement and Women's March [23]

In Minneapolis, businesses closed, students walked out of classes, and Bruce Springsteen performed at a Concert of Solidarity and Resistance at First Avenue. [24] Hundreds of protesters braved frigid winter temperatures, chanting "ICE out!" and marching through major cities including Los Angeles, San Francisco, and New York City. [25]

**2. Corporate ICE Boycott Movement**

The Buc-ee's hoax rode the wave of legitimate corporate accountability campaigns:

**"Resist and Unsubscribe" Campaign:**
NYU professor Scott Galloway launched a major tech boycott in late January 2026, targeting companies that "have been enablers" of ICE. [26] The campaign urged consumers to cancel subscriptions and avoid supporting companies with documented ICE ties, describing economic boycotts as a "powerful weapon that has been hiding in plain sight." [27]

**QuitGPT Movement:**
Gathered over 200,000 sign-ups since late January, protesting OpenAI's ICE-related contracts. U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement uses a resume screening tool powered by ChatGPT-4 as part of its enforcement operations. [28]

**Documented ICE Contractors (Real Targets):**
- **Palantir Technologies:** $30 million contract for "ImmigrationOS" surveillance platform; over $113 million in federal funds since Trump's second term began; $80+ million in ICE-specific contracts [29][30][31]
- **Amazon (AWS):** Part of ICE's nearly $100 million five-year cloud contract [32]
- **Microsoft Azure:** $20 million direct ICE contract, plus participation in cloud infrastructure deals [33]
- **AT&T, Dell, FedEx:** Under scrutiny for large ICE contracts [34]

**Avelo Airline Victory:**
In January 2026, Avelo Airlines—the only scheduled passenger airline providing ICE Air network services—announced it would cut ties with ICE after months of public protests. [35]

**Spotify Victory:**
At the end of 2025, Spotify confirmed it stopped running recruitment ads for ICE after major artists withdrew their music from the platform. [36]

**3. Exploiting Real Political Donations**

The hoax gained plausibility because Arch Aplin **does** have a documented history of major political donations to Texas Republicans:

**Verified Donations (OpenSecrets & Transparency USA):** [37][38]
- **Gov. Greg Abbott:** $575,000 (largest recipient)
- **Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick:** $250,000
- **Attorney General Ken Paxton:** Multiple donations over the years
- **Rep. Randy Weber (R-TX-14):** Multiple donations
- **Rep. Dade Phelan:** Multiple donations
- **Gov. Ron DeSantis (FL):** 2022 donation
- **Former President George W. Bush:** 2000 donation
- **Former President Barack Obama:** 2004 donation

Nonprofit government transparency group OpenSecrets tracked nearly **100 donations** made by Aplin and Buc-ee's executives to politicians, political candidates, and organizations across a nearly **30-year timeline** from 1996 to 2024. [39]

In 2021 alone, just the first 6 months, Arch Aplin donated **$485,000 to Republicans in Texas**, including another $250,000 to Greg Abbott. [40]

**The Hoax Strategy:**
By targeting a CEO with known conservative political donations during a period of heightened ICE enforcement under a Republican administration, the hoax creators exploited the *plausibility gap*—the claim felt believable even without evidence.

**4. The Confusion Factor — Real $1M Donation to Flood Relief**

Buc-ee's **did** donate $1 million in 2025—but to **flood relief**, not ICE:

**The Actual Donation:** [41][42][43]
- **Amount:** $1 million
- **Recipient:** Community Foundation of the Texas Hill Country
- **Purpose:** Relief efforts after devastating July 4, 2025 floods that killed over 130 people in the Texas Hill Country
- **Presentation:** Arch "Beaver" Aplin III presented the donation on NBC's *Today Show* in August 2025
- **Al Roker's Statement:** Called it "the biggest donation ever seen in the history of the segment's 23 years" [44]

**Broader Relief Efforts:**
Buc-ee's also partnered with country musician Robert Earl Keen for a benefit concert called "Applause for the Cause" on August 28, 2025, at Whitewater Amphitheater in New Braunfels. The event raised **$3 million** for the Community Foundation of the Texas Hill Country. [45] The concert featured special performances from Tyler Childers, Miranda Lambert, Jon Randall, Jack Ingram, and tributes to Charlie Robison. [46]

**Total Flood Relief:**
The Community Foundation of the Texas Hill Country ultimately raised **$60 million** for those impacted by the catastrophic July 4 floods through its Kerr County Flood Relief Fund. [47]

**How Confusion Helped the Hoax:**
The existence of a real $1 million Buc-ee's donation in 2025 likely contributed to the hoax's spread. Social media users may have encountered legitimate news about the flood relief donation and, seeing subsequent posts about an alleged ICE donation, failed to distinguish between the two.

### Evidence of Falsehood

**1. Official Company Denial**

Buc-ee's General Counsel Jeff Nadalo issued multiple statements:

> "The claim is entirely unfounded. Neither Beaver nor Buc-ee's has made any donations to ICE." [48]

> "The rumors circulating about Beaver donating to ICE are entirely unfounded and false. Neither Beaver nor anyone associated with Buc-ee's has made any donations..." [49]

> "The circulating rumors about Beaver donating to ICE are entirely false. Neither the company nor anyone associated with Buc-ee's has made any donations to ICE." [50]

**2. Zero Public Records**

Despite the traction the claims received, "the origin of the controversy remains a mystery. An exhaustive search of public records and social media trends failed to identify a specific catalyst or piece of evidence that triggered the allegations." [51]

**Key Record Searches Conducted:**
- **FEC (Federal Election Commission) filings:** No ICE donations
- **Transparency USA (Texas state donations):** No ICE donations
- **OpenSecrets donor database:** No ICE donations
- **Government contract databases:** No Buc-ee's contracts with ICE
- **Corporate SEC filings:** Buc-ee's is privately held, but political/agency donations are public record—none found

**3. Private Company Structure**

Buc-ee's remains a privately held entity. Aplin co-owns the company with Don Wasek, and the pair operates the brand without outside investors. This private structure means the company is not required to disclose the same level of financial data as public corporations, **though political or agency-level donations are typically a matter of public record**. [52] No such ICE donation appears in any public record.

## Key Findings

### What We Know For Certain

1. **The Claim is Fabricated:** No evidence exists in public records, FEC filings, government contracts, or corporate disclosures that Buc-ee's or Arch Aplin donated to ICE.

2. **Patient Zero Identified:** Instagram accounts @floridafoos and @mexicanworldstarfoo originated the claim without sources or evidence.

3. **Massive Viral Spread:** One post reached 283,000+ likes; another 90,000+ likes; spread across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, X.

4. **Official Denial:** Buc-ee's General Counsel Jeff Nadalo categorically denied the claim multiple times.

5. **Real Donation Confused:** Buc-ee's donated $1 million to Texas Hill Country flood relief (August 2025), not ICE.

6. **Political Context Exploited:** Hoax emerged during peak anti-ICE activism following Minneapolis killings and national strikes.

7. **Real Political Donations Exist:** Aplin donated $575,000 to Greg Abbott, $250,000 to Dan Patrick, nearly 100 political donations over 30 years—but ZERO to ICE.

### Why This Matters

**Weaponization of Legitimate Activism:**
The hoax hijacked real corporate accountability campaigns targeting companies like Palantir ($30M ImmigrationOS contract), Microsoft ($20M ICE contract), and Amazon (AWS cloud infrastructure). By fabricating a claim against Buc-ee's, the hoax creators diluted the credibility of legitimate boycott movements.

**Economic Harm Potential:**
With 283,000+ likes and widespread calls for boycotts, the hoax posed real economic threats to Buc-ee's, a privately held company that employs thousands across Texas and the Southern U.S.

**Erosion of Information Integrity:**
The hoax demonstrated how quickly fabricated claims can achieve viral velocity in politically charged environments, outpacing fact-checking and official denials.

**Exploitation of Tragedy:**
The deaths of Renée Good and Alex Pretti sparked legitimate grief and activism. The Buc-ee's hoax exploited this tragedy to spread misinformation for unclear motives—whether clout-chasing, political sabotage, or simple trolling.
