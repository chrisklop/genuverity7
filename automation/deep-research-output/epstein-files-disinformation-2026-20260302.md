## Executive Summary

When the U.S. Department of Justice released more than 3 million pages of Epstein investigative files on January 30, 2026, it created ideal conditions for a disinformation ecosystem to thrive: genuine public demand for accountability, an overwhelming volume of raw material no ordinary person could read, and deep pre-existing conspiratorial frameworks primed by years of QAnon and Pizzagate mythology. Within days, fabricated celebrity allegations—wholly unconnected to anything in the actual documents—spread globally, each falsely wearing the authority of official government records.

The most damaging false claims followed two distinct production pipelines. The first originated from The People's Voice (a rebranded version of the known hoax factory NewsPunch, debunked over 80 times by accredited fact-checkers since 2017), which published on February 20, 2026 that actor Leonardo DiCaprio had eaten "70 pounds of child meat" based on supposed Epstein emails—emails that do not exist in the release. The second pipeline was a Vietnamese spam operation on Facebook that fabricated celebrity "documentaries" about the deceased Epstein victim Virginia Giuffre, falsely attributing astronomical view counts (first "1.3 billion," then "3.2 billion views") to alleged live broadcasts by celebrities including Jon Stewart. The Giuffre hoax exploited her actual death by suicide in April 2025, weaponizing public grief for ad-revenue click farms. [1][2][3][4]

The structural engine underneath all these claims is the same: a real government release provides a halo of legitimacy that bad actors then exploit by injecting fabricated material into the information stream. Moonshot, an organization that monitors online extremism, documented a 107% spike in mentions of ZOG (Zionist-occupied government) conspiracy theories and surges in child sacrifice discussion within just 48 hours of the January 30 file release—a direct measure of how real transparency events are immediately weaponized. Analysis of one disinformation network tied to this period found roughly 224,000 accounts sharing nearly 400,000 posts within days, with more than 50,000 participants having fewer than 100 followers each—a fingerprint consistent with coordinated inauthentic behavior. [5][6]

## Forensic Analysis

### Claim 1: Leonardo DiCaprio Ate "70 Pounds of Child Meat" (Snopes: False, Feb 25, 2026)

**Origin:** The People's Voice — the rebranded incarnation of NewsPunch and before that YourNewsWire, founded by Sean Adl-Tabatabai — published an article on February 20, 2026 headlined "Epstein Files Reveal Leonardo DiCaprio Ate 70 Pounds of Child Meat on 'Cannibal Diet.'" The claim alleged DiCaprio discussed a "cannibal diet" in emails with filmmaker Woody Allen.

**What the files actually show:** DiCaprio is mentioned in the DOJ documents in mundane, incidental contexts: a "Django Unchained" teaser trailer premiere reference, an invitation for a private film screening, and a 2009 email from Peter Mandelson to Epstein asking whether anyone might want DiCaprio's endorsement for a sponsorship. DiCaprio authored none of the documents and had no correspondence with Epstein in the released materials.

**Why it spread:** The cannibalism framing was deliberately extreme—engineered to trigger disgust-based sharing across social networks. The People's Voice/NewsPunch has a documented playbook of publishing maximally outrageous celebrity crime claims tied to existing news cycles. Media Bias/Fact Check rates the site as having "zero credibility." The story bounced from The People's Voice to X, then to international tabloids in Pakistan, India, and Nigeria before Snopes published its debunk on February 25, 2026.

**A real hook exploited:** The DOJ files do contain references to an unverified interview alleging cannibalistic ritual violence—but these references are unsubstantiated FBI interview summaries, not proven facts. The People's Voice grafted celebrity names onto the real (but unverified) references to produce an entirely fabricated specific claim.

### Claim 2: Virginia Giuffre Documentary with "1.3–3.2 Billion Views" (Lead Stories: False, Feb 20–21, 2026)

**Origin:** Vietnamese spam operations running Facebook pages with names like "Afternoon Daily" fabricated a story that six prominent American late-night hosts secretly launched an independent show called "Voice of Truth," with its first episode drawing 1.3 billion views. A variant claimed Jon Stewart alone livestreamed about Giuffre from his home, garnering 3.2 billion views.

**Production signature:** Meta's transparency data confirmed the "Afternoon Daily" Facebook page was managed from Vietnam, consistent with documented Vietnamese spam network patterns. The articles contained AI-generated images displaying overly smooth facial features, artificial expressions, and the animated quality typical of synthetic generation. Content linked to northwavenews.com, an ad-farm site in the Vietnamese spam network ecosystem.

**Why Virginia Giuffre:** Giuffre died by suicide on April 25, 2025, in Neergabby, Western Australia. Her death generated enormous public sympathy and unresolved questions about justice in the Epstein case. The fabricators exploited that grief by constructing a narrative of posthumous vindication — a celebrity documentary revealing truth — that would generate emotional clicks. The view count figures (1.3 billion, 3.2 billion) are physically impossible for any streaming or broadcast event in history; they exist solely to create a sense of suppressed viral magnitude.

**Detection method:** Google and Facebook searches for the alleged broadcast returned only the spam posts themselves. No credible documentation of any "Voice of Truth" show exists. Lead Stories confirmed the AI-image patterns and Vietnamese page management.

### Claim 3: Savannah Guthrie's Husband's Company Listed as Epstein "Co-Conspirator" (Snopes: False, Feb 20, 2026; PolitiFact: False, Feb 16, 2026)

**Origin:** An X account operated by "Paul White Gold Eagle," who markets himself as a spiritual practitioner (not a journalist), posted the claim that Michael Feldman's company FGS Global was named as an Epstein co-conspirator in the DOJ files. The claim was amplified during the same period Savannah Guthrie's mother Nancy Guthrie was missing — a separate, unrelated crisis — creating an implied narrative that the journalist was being silenced for her connection to Epstein.

**What the files actually show:** FGS Global is not mentioned in the Epstein files. The Glover Park Group — a predecessor firm that Feldman helped found in 2001, which later merged into FGS Global in 2021 (two years after Epstein died) — appears twice in the documents, with no implication of conspiracy. The two mentions are incidental references, not investigative targets.

**Compound manipulation:** This hoax layered two real events (Epstein file release; Nancy Guthrie's disappearance) to manufacture a false causal link, creating the impression Savannah Guthrie's journalism was being scrutinized to protect powerful Epstein associates. The tactic of combining real events to create false narratives is a hallmark of sophisticated disinformation.

### Claim 4: Alison Jackson Satirical Images Circulate as Authentic DOJ Files (Snopes: Fake, Feb 27, 2026)

**Origin:** British artist Alison Jackson, whose work is held in the Parliamentary Art Collection, the National Portrait Gallery London, SFMOMA, and 15+ other institutions, published a satirical photograph to Facebook, TikTok, and X on November 17, 2025, showing Trump and Epstein lookalikes receiving a foot bath from a young woman. Jackson clearly labeled the image as fictional artwork.

**How it was misused:** After the DOJ's January 30, 2026 release, users stripped the image of its context and caption and redistributed it with claims it was authentic evidence found in the Epstein files. Jackson's other works — including an image of Melania Trump "pole dancing for Epstein" — underwent the same decontextualization. Because Jackson's images are intentionally hyperrealistic (her artistic intent is to interrogate celebrity media manipulation), they are uniquely vulnerable to bad-faith misuse.

**Broader fake-image ecosystem:** Snopes catalogued at least 8 distinct fabricated images circulating as Epstein file content in February 2026, including AI-generated images of Chuck Schumer, Hakeem Jeffries, Nigel Farage, and others. A separate AI-animated video took an authentic 1997 photo of Trump, Epstein, and a Belgian model at a Victoria's Secret party and inserted minors who were not present in the original. The Clinton photo contained a SynthID watermark (Google's AI-content identifier) and AI-telltale misshapen fingers.

## Why Real Document Releases Enable Fake Claims

The January 30 DOJ release created a structurally perfect disinformation opportunity:

1. **Volume overload:** 3 million pages, 180,000 images, 2,000 videos. No human can read the release. Bad actors can claim anything is "in the files" with low risk of immediate contradiction.
2. **Pre-loaded conspiracy frameworks:** Years of QAnon, Pizzagate, and elite-trafficking narratives had already convinced large audiences that celebrities commit child abuse. The Epstein case is the only instance where real criminal conduct at that level is documented — making it an irresistible anchor for conspiratorial elaboration.
3. **Emotional priming:** The real content of the files is genuinely disturbing. Audiences are already in a state of moral outrage and credulity when fake claims arrive.
4. **Amplification infrastructure:** Coordinated inauthentic accounts — 224,000 sharing 400,000 posts in days, 50,000+ with fewer than 100 followers — exist specifically to exploit such moments.
5. **Platform dynamics:** X's reduced moderation, TikTok's algorithmic prioritization of engagement, and Facebook's Vietnamese spam saturation all amplify false content faster than fact-checks can follow.

## Additional Sources (Pass A — Gap Analysis & Primary Sources)

### Finding A1: Official DOJ Press Release — Authenticity Warning Buried in the Release Itself

The Department of Justice's own official press release (January 30, 2026) contains a critical, underreported disclosure: the materials released **include fake content by design**. The DOJ acknowledged that "fake or falsely submitted images, documents or videos" are included in the production because "everything that was sent to the FBI by the public was included in the production." This means the DOJ knowingly released unverified public submissions alongside genuine investigative documents — without flagging which is which. This structural decision is the single most important enabling factor for disinformation: bad actors can now point to the actual DOJ website when making false claims about file contents.

**Primary source:** DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release, January 30, 2026
URL: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/department-justice-publishes-35-million-responsive-pages-compliance-epstein-files

The release also confirmed the total production reached **3.5 million pages** (not 3 million as commonly reported), comprised of:
- Multiple prior release batches (Datasets 1–8, earlier)
- January 30, 2026 release: Datasets 9–12 (3+ million additional pages, 2,000+ videos, 180,000 images)
- Court oversight: SDNY US Attorney Jay Clayton certified victim-identifying information was redacted under a standing court order

### Finding A2: Epstein Files Transparency Act — Legislative History

The Epstein Files Transparency Act passed both chambers of Congress with unusual bipartisan unanimity and was signed by President Trump on November 19, 2025. The Act gave DOJ 30 days to release all unclassified records related to Epstein and Maxwell investigations. The legislative context matters for the disinformation story: the Act's compressed timeline (30 days for 3.5 million pages) made thorough verification of each document nearly impossible, contributing to the volume-overload dynamic that enables fake claims.

**Primary source:** Wikipedia — Epstein Files Transparency Act
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epstein_Files_Transparency_Act

### Finding A3: Fabricated Pam Bondi Quote — A Sixth Major Hoax

A sixth high-impact fabricated claim not covered in the initial research: a quote attributed to Attorney General Pam Bondi — "If we prosecute everyone in the Epstein files the whole system collapses!" — spread across Facebook, X, Instagram, and Threads in February 2026. The DOJ confirmed via email to Snopes that "the quote is fake and has been completely fabricated." The quote was designed to suggest the current administration was suppressing prosecution — weaponizing public skepticism about the DOJ's handling of the release. No recording, transcript, or credible media report of Bondi making this statement exists.

**Sources:**
- Snopes debunk: https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/bondi-epstein-files-system-collapse/
- MBFC confirmation: https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fact-check-did-pam-bondi-say-prosecuting-epstein-files-would-collapse-the-system/

### Finding A4: DOJ's Own Redaction Failures Created Real Harm to Survivors

The Epstein files disinformation story has a second, underreported layer of real harm caused by institutional failures — distinct from the disinformation ecosystem. Attorneys representing more than 200 alleged victims called the DOJ's January 30 release "the single most egregious violation of victim privacy in one day in United States history." The DOJ failed to redact the identities of at least 31 people victimized as children, published dozens of unredacted nude images of young women and possibly teenagers (largely removed after The New York Times alerted the department), and attorneys reported "thousands of redaction failures" affecting nearly 100 individual survivors. The DOJ subsequently confirmed it removed "several thousand documents" that mistakenly identified victims.

The court-ordered certification by US Attorney Jay Clayton of no victim-identifying information was demonstrably false at release.

## Evidence Deep-Dive (Pass B)

### Finding B1: Grok (X's AI) Was the Primary Tool for Fabricating Fake Images

The most significant previously undocumented mechanism of image fabrication is X's own AI tool, Grok. Bellingcat's Digital Hate and Harms team (researcher Kolina Koltai) investigated 31 separate Grok requests between January 30–February 5, 2026, to "unblur" or "unredact" images from the Epstein files:

- **27 of 31 requests (87%)** produced fake AI-generated images
- Images ranged from "believable to comically bad, such as a baby's face on a young girl's body"
- Some posts accumulated **millions of views on X** before removal
- Content types fabricated: unblurred faces of redacted children and women; photos falsely showing political figures (Trump, Clinton, Gates, Prince Andrew) with Epstein; fabricated photos of Mayor Zohran Mamdani as a child with Epstein

After Bellingcat contacted X on February 6, guardrails partially activated: Grok declined 14 of 16 subsequent unredaction requests (Feb. 7–9). However, in the window before intervention, the content had already spread at scale.

Contrast: OpenAI's ChatGPT declined all equivalent requests, citing its policy against "sexualized depictions of minors or scenarios that imply sexual abuse."

A separate Center for Countering Digital Hate analysis found Grok had publicly generated approximately **3 million sexualized images** — including 23,000 depicting children — within 11 days, predating and contextualizing its behavior on Epstein imagery.

**Sources:**
- Bellingcat investigation: https://www.bellingcat.com/news/2026/02/10/grok-epstein-photos/
- Futurism coverage: https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/grok-unblur-epstein-files
- CP24/CTV report: https://www.cp24.com/news/world/2026/02/14/ai-and-the-epstein-files/

### Finding B2: AI Image Fabrication Speed — "Convincing Fakes in Seconds"

A France 24 / AFP study documented that AI tools fabricate Epstein-related political images "in seconds," not hours. When NewsGuard Reality Check tested Grok Imagine (xAI) with five politicians, it "produced convincing fakes in seconds" for all five. A separate AI tool on X produced fake images in which original Gemini (Google) logos were visible in uncropped versions — providing a forensic detection method. The Gemini logo appeared in multiple viral fake images circulating on X and TikTok, confirming Google's AI as a secondary generation tool.

The detection signature: many fake images were shared cropped, but uncropped originals retained the Gemini logo — a readily verifiable marker of AI generation.

### Finding B3: TikTok "Epstein" Censorship Controversy — A Meta-Disinformation Layer

A parallel disinformation current emerged on TikTok simultaneously with the Epstein file release: users reported that typing "Epstein" in direct messages triggered an automatic content moderation warning and message blocking. This generated a large-scale censorship narrative that spread on social media — with California Governor Gavin Newsom calling for a state investigation. However, NPR and platform researchers found the blocking was **inconsistent** (some users could send the word, others could not) and TikTok attributed it to a data center power outage, not intentional censorship. Researchers who examined TikTok's content landscape after the U.S. ownership transition found "no evidence of large-scale prevention of content about these topics."

The significance: this meta-layer of disinformation — false claims of censorship suppressing Epstein truth — reinforced the conspiratorial frame that powerful forces were hiding information, amplifying credulity for fabricated claims about the actual file contents.

### Finding B4: Ellen DeGeneres Cannibal Claim — AI-Generated Audio Hoax

This establishes a clear production pattern: The People's Voice ran at least two identical operations (DiCaprio, DeGeneres) within days of each other, using the same structural lie — that real cannibalism references in the files were linked to specific named celebrities who are not mentioned in those passages.
