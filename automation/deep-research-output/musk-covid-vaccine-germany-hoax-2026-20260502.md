## Executive Summary

In April 2026, a false claim that COVID-19 vaccines killed between 20,000 and 60,000 Germans achieved explosive global reach after Elon Musk amplified it to his 237 million followers on X. The claim originated in testimony given on March 19, 2026, by Dr. Helmut Sterz — a retired veterinarian and former Pfizer Europe toxicologist who left the company in 2007 — before Germany's Bundestag Corona Enquete Commission, where he had been invited to speak by the far-right AfD parliamentary faction. Sterz applied a discredited "underreporting multiplier" of 30 to raw passive surveillance figures from the Paul-Ehrlich-Institut (PEI), Germany's vaccine safety authority, inflating 2,133 reported deaths (temporally proximate to vaccination, not confirmed as causally linked) into a projected death toll of approximately 60,000. His methodology, experts have noted, "confuses coincidence with causation, misuses passive surveillance data, and is not supported by the best available scientific evidence." [1] [2]

Far-right Swedish influencer Peter Imanuelsen, who goes by the handle PeterSweden, posted Sterz's claims on April 12, 2026, framing them as a bombshell admission from "a Pfizer insider who was former head of toxicology in Europe." Elon Musk reposted the content within minutes, adding his own personal anecdote: "my second vaccine shot almost sent me to the hospital. Felt like I was dying. The vaccine dosage was obviously too high and done too many times." [1] Within 30 hours, Musk's post had accumulated more than 50 million views; by the time FactCheck.org published its debunking on April 16, both posts had collectively reached over 120 million views. [1] [3]

The actual data from the Paul-Ehrlich-Institut shows a radically different picture. Of more than 197 million COVID-19 vaccine doses administered in Germany between December 27, 2020 and December 31, 2024, the PEI recorded 2,133 deaths reported after Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination. However, the PEI explicitly states these reports "do not automatically indicate that there is a causal relationship." Following individual causality assessment, only 28 of those 2,133 deaths were classified as having a "possible or probable" causal relationship with vaccination — a rate of approximately 0.00002% per dose. [1] [4] Pfizer confirmed Sterz "was not working at Pfizer during the pandemic or during the decade preceding it, and consequently had no involvement in the development of the COVID-19 vaccine." [1] Germany's Health Minister Karl Lauterbach, who was present at the Bundestag hearing, rejected Sterz's claims as false on the spot. [2] [5]

## Forensic Analysis

### 1. Origin: The Sterz Testimony (March 19, 2026)

The misinformation chain begins with a formal parliamentary setting designed to confer legitimacy. Germany's Bundestag Corona Enquete Commission — a parliamentary body tasked with reviewing Germany's COVID-19 response — held a public hearing on March 19, 2026, focused on health system performance, vaccination strategy, and research. [6] Among the witnesses called was Dr. Helmut Sterz, invited by the AfD, Germany's far-right political party.

Sterz, born in 1946 and a veterinarian by training with a doctorate in virology from Justus Liebig University in Giessen, worked for pharmaceutical companies including Roche and Pfizer over a 35-year career. He headed Pfizer's European toxicology centers in Amboise (France) and Sandwich (UK). He retired in 2007 — more than 13 years before the COVID-19 pandemic and the development of the Comirnaty vaccine he criticized. Following retirement, Sterz authored a book titled "Die Impf-Mafia" ("The Vaccine Mafia") in which he argues COVID-19 vaccines were unlawfully approved. [7] [8]

In his testimony, Sterz made two central claims:

**Claim A — Skipped Safety Studies:** He alleged that 10 essential preclinical safety studies were bypassed during the development of the Pfizer/BioNTech Comirnaty vaccine, including long-term carcinogenicity studies and reproductive toxicity assessments, citing "time constraints" imposed by the pandemic emergency authorization process.

**Claim B — Mass Death Estimate:** He cited the Paul-Ehrlich-Institut's passive surveillance data — approximately 2,133 deaths reported following Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination — and applied a "30x underreporting factor" borrowed from U.S. Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) methodology, arriving at an estimate of 20,000 to 60,000 COVID-19 vaccine deaths in Germany.

German Health Minister Karl Lauterbach, present at the hearing, publicly rejected both claims. The European Medicines Agency separately confirmed that residual DNA found in some manufacturing batches — another of Sterz's concerns — is "minimal, fragmented, and within safety thresholds." [5] [9]

### 2. Mechanism of Distortion: The Underreporting Multiplier Problem

Sterz's death estimate rests on a methodological error that has repeatedly fueled vaccine misinformation across multiple countries and years. Passive pharmacovigilance systems like the PEI database and the U.S. VAERS collect voluntary reports of adverse events that occur after vaccination. These systems are specifically designed to detect signals for further investigation — they are not designed to establish or quantify causation.

By design and explicit disclaimer, every regulatory agency that operates such systems — including the PEI and the U.S. FDA — states that adverse event reports "do not automatically indicate that there is a causal relationship." [4] A temporal association (a death occurring after vaccination) does not indicate a vaccine caused the death. Particularly among the elderly and chronically ill populations who received early COVID-19 vaccine doses, deaths from unrelated causes (cardiovascular disease, cancer, dementia) were statistically expected to occur near the time of vaccination simply because those populations have high background mortality rates.

The "30x multiplier" Sterz applied derives from a Harvard Pilgrim Health Care study frequently cited by anti-vaccine activists, which estimated that fewer than 1% of adverse events may be reported in passive surveillance systems. However, this estimate:

- Was designed for general adverse event underreporting, not for fatal events specifically (more serious events are typically more likely to be reported)
- Does not account for the substantial "overreporting" or "notoriety bias" that occurred during the COVID-19 vaccine rollout, when any death after vaccination received intense public scrutiny and media attention
- Cannot be validly transferred from the U.S. healthcare context to Germany's more robust pharmacovigilance infrastructure
- Treats all reported deaths as vaccine-caused, which contradicts the explicit purpose of the reporting system

As biostatistician Jeffrey S. Morris, director of the biostatistics division at the University of Pennsylvania, stated directly: "It is not valid to presume the 2,000 reported deaths were caused by vaccines." [1]

Epidemiologist Dr. Mahmoud Zureik, director of EPI-PHARE and professor at the University of Paris-Saclay, summarized the fatal flaw: the methodology "confuses coincidence with causation, misuses passive surveillance data, and is not supported by the best available scientific evidence." [1]

### 3. Amplification Pipeline: From Fringe to Global

**Stage 1 — Fringe Amplification (Late March 2026):** Following the March 19 testimony, Sterz's claims were initially picked up by fringe and anti-vaccine media outlets. His book "Die Impf-Mafia" was already available on Amazon Germany, providing a pre-existing audience. Posts on X from accounts like "@toobaffled" amplified the Bundestag testimony within days, but without reaching mainstream audiences. [10]

**Stage 2 — Imanuelsen Posts (April 12, 2026):** Peter Imanuelsen, a Swedish influencer who goes by PeterSweden and has a substantial far-right following, published a post on X on April 12, 2026, framing the claims in deliberately alarming terms: "A Pfizer insider who was former head of toxicology in Europe has just come out and said something that many 'conspiracy theorists' suspected... He estimates that 20,000 to 60,000 people in Germany have died from the c*vid vaccine. This should be headline news EVERYWHERE." [1] Imanuelsen's characterization misrepresented Sterz's employment (he retired in 2007) and his role in the vaccine's development (he had none).

**Stage 3 — Musk Amplification (April 12, 2026):** Elon Musk reposted Imanuelsen's content within minutes, adding a personal narrative that lent further credibility to the false claim: "The vaccine dosage was obviously too high and done too many times...my second vaccine shot almost sent me to the hospital. Felt like I was dying." [1] [3] Musk's 237 million followers on X meant this content was algorithmically amplified to an audience orders of magnitude larger than Imanuelsen's original reach.

**Stage 4 — Global Spread:** Within approximately 30 hours, Musk's post had accumulated over 50.3 million views and 255,000 likes. Imanuelsen's original reached 64 million views. [3] The combined 120+ million impressions created a self-reinforcing cycle as pro-vaccine-skeptic media outlets (including Children's Health Defense, David Icke, SGT Report, NaturalNews, Global Research, and others) republished and amplified the claims without any critical analysis. [11] [12] [13]

### 4. Debunking and Official Response

FactCheck.org published its debunking on April 16, 2026, updated April 24, 2026. Key findings:
- PEI assessed only 28 out of 2,133 reported deaths as having "possible or probable" causal relationship with the Pfizer/BioNTech vaccine [1]
- Pfizer confirmed Sterz had no involvement in COVID-19 vaccine development [1]
- Multiple large-scale studies show vaccinated populations had *lower* mortality than unvaccinated populations [1]

The Paul-Ehrlich-Institut issued a direct statement to fact-checkers: there is "no evidence" for the claim that the Pfizer/BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine killed 60,000 people in Germany, and the 2,133 reported deaths cited by Sterz represent an "inappropriate basis for such an estimate." [1]

Neither Musk nor Imanuelsen issued corrections.

### PEI Data in Full Detail

The Paul-Ehrlich-Institut's December 2024 pharmacovigilance report — the document at the center of this entire misinformation episode — provides far more granular data than Sterz's testimony conveyed. The primary PEI news page [4] and official PDF report [17] confirm the following:

**Doses and reports (December 27, 2020 – December 31, 2024):**
- Total COVID-19 doses administered in Germany: **197,033,944**
- Total suspected adverse event reports: **350,868**
- Serious adverse event reports: **63,909** (18.2% of all reports)
- Adverse event reporting rate: **1.78 per 1,000 doses**
- Serious adverse event rate: **0.32 per 1,000 doses**

**On reported deaths specifically:**
- Deaths reported following Pfizer/BioNTech vaccination (all temporally proximate, not confirmed causal): **2,133** [1]
- Deaths assessed by PEI as having "possible or probable" causal relationship with the Pfizer/BioNTech vaccine: **28** [1]
- Deaths assessed across ALL COVID-19 vaccines combined as having probable causal relationship (restrictive causality assessment): **74** [search results citing European Society of Medicine analysis of PEI data]

The PEI report states explicitly: "Spontaneous reports of suspected adverse events are important for the timely detection of new safety signals. However, apart from exceptions such as local reactions at the vaccination site or anaphylactic reactions immediately after vaccination, **spontaneous reports are not suitable for determining if the reported adverse reaction was caused by vaccination or how frequently it occurs.**" [17]

The PEI also noted it "intends to cease the preparation and publication of the regular safety reports on COVID-19 vaccine products in its current form" — having declared no new safety signals since March 31, 2023. [17]

**The 74-death figure explained:** The distinction between 28 (Pfizer/BioNTech-specific probable causal deaths) and 74 (all COVID-19 vaccines combined probable causal deaths) is important context. The 74 figure, cited in FactCheck.org's research and traceable to PEI methodology, represents the restrictive causality assessment across all approved COVID-19 vaccines used in Germany — including Moderna, Johnson & Johnson, and AstraZeneca — administered over four years and 197 million doses.

### The 30x Underreporting Multiplier: Methodology Critique in Full

The "30x underreporting factor" Sterz applied originates from a Harvard Pilgrim Health Care study (ESP:VAERS, AHRQ grant R18 HS 017045) that used automated electronic medical record scanning in a single Massachusetts-based health system covering 376,452 patients between 2006 and 2009. [23] The study found that clinicians submitted VAERS reports for approximately 0.69 per 1,000 vaccine doses — suggesting substantial underreporting of detected events relative to what automated scanning identified.

However, anti-vaccine activists and Sterz specifically have misapplied this study in multiple compounding ways:

1. **Wrong system, wrong country:** The Harvard Pilgrim study examined U.S. VAERS, not Germany's PEI pharmacovigilance system. Germany operates a more centralized, physician-reporting-oriented system than the U.S. voluntary consumer-reporting VAERS model. Reporting rates and detected-event ratios are not transferable between the two systems.

2. **General events vs. fatal events:** VAERS explicitly acknowledges that "more serious and unexpected medical events are probably more likely to be reported than minor ones, especially when they occur soon after vaccination." [14] Deaths — the most serious possible outcome — are subject to higher reporting completeness than minor side effects. Applying a "fewer than 1% reported" figure derived from *all* adverse events to *fatal* events specifically is methodologically invalid.

3. **Notoriety bias during the COVID vaccine rollout:** The COVID-19 vaccine program occurred under unprecedented media scrutiny. Any death following vaccination — regardless of cause — received intense public attention and was more likely to be reported to pharmacovigilance systems than deaths in ordinary medical contexts. This "notoriety bias" suggests *overreporting* relative to baseline, not underreporting by a factor of 30. The PEI spokesperson explicitly stated that applying "a fixed underreporting factor" lacks a valid "data basis" and that PEI "takes various underreporting scenarios into account" in its own signal detection methodology. [1]

4. **Treats temporal correlation as causation:** The Harvard Pilgrim study estimated underreporting of *detected* adverse events — events that had already been clinically evaluated and identified as potentially related to a vaccine. Sterz applied the multiplier to *all temporally proximate deaths*, the majority of which were never assessed as vaccine-related.

**Expert consensus on Sterz's methodology:**

Dr. Mahmoud Zureik, director of EPI-PHARE and professor at the University of Paris-Saclay, stated the approach "confuses coincidence with causation, misuses passive surveillance data, and is not supported by the best available scientific evidence," and called the factor of 30 "arbitrary." [1]

Dr. Jeffrey S. Morris, director of the biostatistics division at the University of Pennsylvania, stated: "It is not valid to presume the 2,000 reported deaths were caused by vaccines, much less to presume that there were 30x this number to arrive at the 60k number the person claims." [1]

The Paul-Ehrlich-Institut spokesperson directly rejected the multiplier approach, stating "the data basis for such a calculation is lacking." [1]
