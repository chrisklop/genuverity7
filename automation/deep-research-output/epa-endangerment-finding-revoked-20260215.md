## Executive Summary

On February 12, 2026, the Trump administration revoked the Environmental Protection Agency's 2009 endangerment finding—the scientific determination that greenhouse gases threaten public health and welfare under the Clean Air Act. [1] President Trump falsely claimed the finding "had no basis in fact, had none whatsoever, and it had no basis in law," [2] directly contradicting established Supreme Court precedent, extensive peer-reviewed science, and multiple federal court rulings that upheld the finding.

The endangerment finding originated from the Supreme Court's 2007 *Massachusetts v. EPA* decision, which ruled 5-4 that greenhouse gases qualify as air pollutants under the Clean Air Act and mandated EPA to determine whether they endanger public health. [3] In December 2009, EPA concluded—after reviewing decades of scientific research from the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), U.S. Climate Change Science Program, U.S. Global Change Research Program, and National Research Council—that six greenhouse gases (CO2, CH4, N2O, HFCs, PFCs, SF6) pose a threat to current and future generations. [4] Industry groups challenged this finding in *Coalition for Responsible Regulation v. EPA* (2012), but the D.C. Circuit Court unanimously upheld it as neither arbitrary nor capricious, and the Supreme Court declined review. [5]

Far from weakening, the scientific evidence has strengthened dramatically since 2009. The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine released a comprehensive assessment in September 2025 concluding that the endangerment finding "has stood the test of time" and is "now reinforced by even stronger evidence." [6] Stanford climate scientists documented that understanding of how global warming influences extreme weather, health impacts, and economic productivity "has increased dramatically in the last decade." [7] Yet the Trump administration cited a discredited July 2025 Department of Energy report written by climate contrarians appointed by Energy Secretary Chris Wright—a report that over 85 scientists condemned in a 400-page rebuttal as "full of errors" and dismissed as lacking scientific merit. [8] The panel was disbanded in September 2025 following widespread scientific criticism. [9]

The revocation eliminates all federal greenhouse gas emission standards for vehicles from model years 2012-2027 and beyond, claiming $1.3 trillion in "savings" to taxpayers. [10] However, experts note this figure ignores tens of billions in annual public health costs from climate-related deaths, extreme weather, and air pollution. Environmental law scholars and 23 state attorneys general have characterized the action as illegal, violating both Supreme Court precedent and EPA's statutory obligations under the Clean Air Act. [11] Massachusetts Attorney General Andrea Joy Campbell stated: "EPA's unlawful rescission shows just how far this Administration will go to grant favors to polluters—ignoring clear Supreme Court precedent, basic facts, and decades of scientific research." [12]

## Forensic Analysis

### Origin: Patient Zero and Initial Spread

**Patient Zero:** President Donald Trump announced the revocation at a White House press conference on February 12, 2026, alongside EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin. Trump's verbatim claim: "Known as the endangerment finding, this determination had no basis in fact, had none whatsoever, and it had no basis in law." [13]

**EPA Administrator's Framing:** Zeldin characterized the action as "the single largest deregulatory action in U.S. history," claiming the finding had been "the source of 16 years of consumer choice restrictions and trillions of dollars in hidden costs for Americans." [14] The EPA's official press release asserted that "Section 202(a) of the CAA does not provide statutory authority for EPA to prescribe motor vehicle and engine emission standards in the manner previously utilized, including for the purpose of addressing global climate change." [15]

**Pre-Announcement Signals:** The revocation was telegraphed days in advance. CNBC reported on February 10, 2026, that "EPA will revoke 'endangerment finding' that underpins all climate regulation this week," allowing fossil fuel advocates and conservative media to prepare supportive messaging. [16]

**Conservative Media Amplification:**

**Fossil Fuel Industry Support:** Industry groups that previously challenged the finding in *Coalition for Responsible Regulation v. EPA* (2012) immediately praised the action, positioning it as correcting federal overreach.

[22]

### Why It Spread: Psychological and Political Drivers

**1. Regulatory Rollback Narrative:** The framing as "largest deregulatory action in history" resonated with small-government advocates and fossil fuel interests who portray climate regulations as economic burdens rather than public health protections.

**2. Science Denial Infrastructure:** The administration relied on the discredited July 2025 Department of Energy Climate Working Group report to provide pseudo-scientific cover. Despite being debunked by 85+ scientists and disbanded in September 2025, the report's existence allowed supporters to claim there was "scientific debate." [23]

**3. Economic Fear-Mongering:** Claims of "$1.3 trillion in savings" and "consumer choice" appealed to voters concerned about electric vehicle transitions and energy costs, though these claims ignored health costs from climate impacts.

**4. Legal Sophistry:** By claiming the Clean Air Act doesn't authorize greenhouse gas regulation "in the manner previously utilized," the EPA created legal ambiguity—despite *Massachusetts v. EPA* (2007) unambiguously establishing this authority. [24]

### Evidence: What the Science Actually Shows

**Supreme Court Mandate:**
*Massachusetts v. EPA* (2007) ruled 5-4 that:
- Greenhouse gases qualify as "air pollutants" under the Clean Air Act
- EPA must determine whether GHGs endanger public health/welfare
- Policy preferences cannot justify refusing to regulate matters within statutory authority [25]

**2009 Endangerment Finding Scientific Basis:**
The EPA's Technical Support Document synthesized findings from:
- **IPCC Assessment Reports** (Fourth Assessment, 2007)
- **U.S. Climate Change Science Program** synthesis reports
- **U.S. Global Change Research Program** national assessments
- **National Research Council** peer-reviewed studies

The finding identified six greenhouse gases as threats based on "decades of scientific research and peer-reviewed assessment reports synthesizing thousands of individual climate science studies." [26]

**Court Validation:**
*Coalition for Responsible Regulation v. EPA* (D.C. Circuit, 2012) unanimously ruled:
- The endangerment finding was "neither arbitrary nor capricious"
- EPA's interpretation of Clean Air Act provisions was "unambiguously correct"
- Industry and state challenges lacked merit [27]

The Supreme Court declined to review this decision, leaving it as binding precedent.

**Strengthened Evidence Since 2009:**
The National Academies September 2025 report documented:
- Scientific evidence has "increased dramatically" for each climate impact area
- Understanding was "uncertain or tentative in 2009" but is now "resolved"
- Evidence is "beyond scientific dispute" [28]

**Stanford Research (2025) documented specific evidence growth:**
- **Heat impacts:** More severe heat waves; rising ER visits on hot days
- **Wildfires:** Larger fires; increased smoke-related deaths
- **Sea level rise:** Accelerating coastal hazards
- **Agriculture:** Growing crop yield reductions
- **Disease:** Expanding climate-linked health problems
- **Conflict:** Climate change influenced ~20% of armed conflict risk over previous century [29]

**Health Costs Ignored:**
A 2021 *Nature Climate Change* study found approximately **9,700 annual heat-related deaths** globally attributable to human-caused climate change across 732 cities—with health costs estimated at minimum **$10 billion annually** in the U.S. alone. [30] The EPA's claimed "$1.3 trillion in savings" ignores these costs entirely.
